This is post number 2 in the series about pregnancy and birth. You can read post number 1 [here](/a-baby-waits-faith-precedes-the-miracle/). A few days after finding out that I was pregnant, [we told the kids](/soybeans-and-vegetables-unit-study/). They were pretty surprised. haha.The baby's womb name became Honey so from then on we referred to the baby as "Baby Honey."

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_4098.jpeg)

We gradually told our family members and some friends and it was a common reaction for them to not believe us at first. Haha. I think everyone was pretty convinced that we must be done having babies since it had been so long since our last one (a 5 year gap). As I mentioned in my last post, Heavenly Father told me that I would see miracles. I clung to that promise. I feel strongly that the faith that we exercised in order to invite this baby into our family was the catalyst for the miracles that we have experienced over the past 10 months. Miracles of health. Miracles of healing. Miracles of peace. Miracles of dreams being realized. Faith precedes miracles.

> “\[F]or it is by faith that miracles are wrought; and it is by faith that angels appear and minister unto men; wherefore, if these things have ceased wo be unto the children of men, for it is because of unbelief, and all is in vain.” ([Moroni 7:37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/7?lang=eng))

> "For if there be no faith among the children of men God can do no miracle among them; wherefore, he showed not himself until after their faith." ([Ether 12:12](https://abn.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12.12?lang=eng))

A couple months into my pregnancy, I decided to start jotting down a quick note in my phone every time I would notice and recognize one of these miracles. Lots of these would seem insignificant to other people but to me, they were miracles. Many of these things were small, but a handful were much bigger. I am certain that there were many, many more that I didn't even recognize or forgot to record but even without those, my list grew to record over 100 miracles throughout my pregnancy. Soon after finding out that I was pregnant, I started feeling symptoms. The first trimester has never been my favorite and this one was my most challenging. The "morning" sickness came on earlier and lasted longer than my other pregnancies. I felt nauseous and fatigued nearly all day, everyday. I laid in my bed a lot... just trying to survive the next few weeks. I realize that many other women have it way worse in their pregnancies, so I really shouldn't complain, but it is what it is. This was Roxanne's prayer one day... "Dear Heavenly Father. Thank Thee for Honey. Please bless that mommy will be safe when she is throwing up in the toilet. In the name of Jesus Christ, amen." haha! At the beginning of October, I went to my first prenatal appointment. After getting my weight, the nurse checked my blood pressure. Of course it was high...like it always was at this doctor's office. I explained that it was always high here but I had been taking it at home several times over the past couple weeks and it was always normal at home. The nurse still said we needed to take it again. ...again, it was high. Then she tried a third time but on the other arm this time... high again. I told her that I had all of the readings from taking it at home and could show them to her. She told me that I could show them to the midwife. I was frustrated and disappointed that my blood pressure had been high again this time but I was grateful that I had been taking it at home and had all good readings. When I met with the midwife, she talked to me about my blood pressure and I had to explain the whole thing... even how this same thing happened when I was pregnant with Roxanne. I showed her my blood pressure readings from home. She agreed that I must just get nervous at the doctor office and everything was probably just fine and normal. She told me to keep taking it at home, but I didn't need to take it very often... even just once a week or so. It wasn't worth causing stress (because obviously that just raises my blood pressure. haha) I got to have a little ultrasound and it was fun to see the baby and see/hear the heartbeat. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_4361.jpeg) I had 2 more appointments at this same doctor/midwife office before we moved across the country. Again, at both of them, my blood pressure was high (but actually went down to a better level during the second check...which was a miracle) but because of monitoring at home, the midwives weren't concerned about it. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_4692.jpeg) 12 weeks One of the biggest miracles that happened during my pregnancy was [finding our farm](/theres-no-place-like-home-in-kansas/). This move across the country couldn't have happened at a better time, as far as my pregnancy is concerned. When we went under contract on our house, I was 12 weeks a long and just starting to feel _some_ relief from the constant nausea and fatigue. Over the next few weeks of packing and moving, miraculously I had enough energy to do what needed to be done. I spent hours everyday packing up our house...and then when we got to our new house, hours everyday unpacking our house. It also worked out perfectly because I wasn't super big yet so the packing and unpacking wasn't quite as challenging as it would have been if I was further along in my pregnancy. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_5621.jpeg) 18 weeks My first appointment at our my new midwife's office was halfway through my pregnancy. Miraculously, my blood pressure readings at this doctors office were always completely normal. I never took this for granted and I always offered up a silent prayer of gratitude to heaven for this. ... at my very last prenatal appointment (2 days before my baby was born), my blood pressure reading was a little on the higher side (not by a ton though) but then when they checked it again at the end of my appointment, it was completely normal. At my first appointment, Jershon and I got to see the baby on the ultrasound. We wanted the gender to be a surprise so we didn't find out during this ultrasound. It was still really fun to see the baby though and reassuring to find out that he/she was perfectly healthy and growing right on track. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_7779.jpeg) When I was 23 weeks along, I began preparing for the birth. ...Not that I thought I would go into labor anytime soon, but I like to give myself a lot of time to prepare since I planned on an unmedicated birth. Let me give a little backstory... When I had my first baby, Landon, I was induced 3 weeks early because of low amniotic fluid. I got an epidural (that was the plan) and everything went well. I even wrote in his birth story about how the epidural was my new best friend. haha. ![](/uploads/2021/05/1936083_696429353009_750127_n.jpeg) Baby Landon When I was pregnant with my second, Porter, I planned on getting an epidural again. Well... Porter had different plans. Although I had been at the hospital all night (after 2 false alarms in the 2 weeks before his birth), not really progressing much, when he decided to come, he came fast. I progressed from 3 cm to 10 cm (and then delivery) in about an hour. Needless to say, I didn't have time to get an epidural. ...I was not prepared for an unmedicated birth. ... When the nurse midwife (who they had pulled in from the hall to deliver Porter since my doctor couldn't make it in time) handed him to me, I couldn't even hold him for a little while. He just laid there on my chest. I couldn't even open my eyes because of the shock that I was still feeling from the intensity of the birth experience. I said to myself "I never want to give birth without an epidural ever again." ![](/uploads/2021/05/DSC_0011-1.jpeg) Baby Porter Fast forward to about 18 months later. We were thinking about trying to get pregnant again. So naturally, I started to think about giving birth again. And I decided that I didn’t want a repeat of Porter’s birth experience. I didn’t want to chance being caught unprepared again. So even though I still didn’t want to give birth naturally (unmedicated), I felt that I should at least put some effort into preparing for it, _just in case_. One day that summer I ran across a book called [_The Gift of Giving Life: Rediscovering the Divine Nature of Pregnancy and Birth_](https://www.amazon.com/Gift-Giving-Life-Rediscovering-Pregnancy/dp/0615622526/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8\&qid=1507778892\&sr=8-1\&keywords=the+gift+of+giving+life). The title of this book interested me so I decided to buy it and read it. When it came in the mail a few days later, I spent pretty much all of my free time reading it. I loved what I was able to learn from that book. It was so inspiring. It is a compilation of birth stories and of essays about pregnancy and birth. The birth stories aren’t all about unmedicated/natural births, but many of them were. As I read this book, I started to feel a desire grow in me. I started to feel that instead of preparing for a natural birth, _just in case_, I was seriously considering having a natural birth the next time _on purpose_. A couple months later, I got pregnant with Evelyn. I had learned from _The Gift of Giving Life_ book about a childbirth method called Hypnobabies and I felt really drawn to this method. So when I was a few months pregnant, I borrowed the Hypnobabies home study program from a friend and practiced the techniques and skills throughout the rest of my pregnancy. Evelyn's birth was an amazing experience. I was able to use the Hypnobabies skills to help me to have a very positive unmedicated/natural birth. I found that being _prepared_ for an unmedicated birth made a world of difference in how the experience went. The skills that I learned even had a very positive effect on the end of my pregnancy with her and also on the postpartum period as well. ![](/uploads/2021/05/DSC_0129.jpeg) Baby Evelyn When I was pregnant with Roxanne, I decided to use the Hypnobabies program again for her birth and again, I had a great experience with having an unmedicated birth. ![](/uploads/2021/05/DSC_1684.jpeg) Baby Roxanne Throughout both of these experiences (and especially in comparing them to my first 2 births), I learned that pregnancy and birth are not just the means to an end. It's not _just_ about getting that baby out of you and into your arms. There is so much more that is offered to women during pregnancy and birth. It can be a transformative experience and can truly be life changing (and not _just_ because you have a baby to take care of now). Of course I still think positively of Landon's birth, but I don't feel the same connection to the _birth experience_ as I do to my other births (and that makes me a little sad when I think about it). Porter's birth was intense but I will be forever grateful for that experience and that it didn't go as I had planned. His birth changed me in significant ways. His birth is what set me on my personal growth journey and has led me to many, many life changing resources (not just about birth.) I feel a special connection with Evelyn and Roxanne's births because they were empowering, ennobling, and strengthening. They helped me to recognize my strengths and powers as a woman. So when I got pregnant with baby #5, I started thinking about the birth. I had some passing thoughts that perhaps I would do something different than the Hypnobabies this time. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_8788.jpeg) 25 weeks At the beginning of this pregnancy, as I thought ahead to the labor/delivery, I planned on using the Hypnobabies program/method again. I had great experiences with this method during Evelyn and Roxanne's pregnancies and births. So why mess with a good thing? But at the same time, I didn't know if I would feel inspired to use a different method this time so I was open to that as well if that's what Heavenly Father wanted me to do. As I was in the middle of my 2nd trimester, and thinking about how I would need to start preparing for the birth sometime soon, I prayed about my Hypnobabies plan, with openness to revelation if Heavenly Father had something else in mind for me. One morning, in January, I was listening to a meditation from the [Small Seed Still app](https://www.thesmallseed.com/pages/app) about Christ being the deliverer. As I was listening, I felt an impression from God. I felt like He was telling me to design my own labor and delivery meditations for this birth - meditations that would help me to focus my mind and heart on the voice of the Lord and His promises to me and how He would help me through the challenges of bringing this baby through the veil and to the Earth. I felt that this impression came in answer to my question that I asked Him in my prayer a few days before when I was talking to Him about my plan to use the Hypnobabies method for this labor/delivery...unless He inspired me to use a different method instead. So here I was... I didn't really know how to design my own meditations, but if this was what the Lord wanted me to do then I trusted that He would inspire and instruct me. This revelation was both exciting, and incredibly daunting. I haven't ever written a meditation script before.... and writing something that I would be relying on keeping me in a peaceful and relaxed mindset during one of the most intense and challenging experiences of my life... felt like a whole lot of pressure. What if I wasn't able to be successful? What if my own meditation script wasn't as helpful as the Hypnobabies scripts have been for me in the past? How in the world do I even write and record a meditation?...especially one that is long enough for my intended purposes? I mean, I planned to have it on repeat (like I had with the Hypnobabies birth track), but I needed it to be at least 30 minutes or so long. At first I did quick google searching to see if there were already some spiritual birth meditations out there that either I could use, or could get some ideas from. I didn't find anything. So I figured that I was just going to have to start from scratch. But even though I had no idea what I was doing or how to even get started, I just trusted that God would lead me to resources and would help me to accomplish what He was impressing me to do. So I started writing. ...and writing... and writing. I spent the next 13 weeks writing the meditation script. Many, many hours were poured into this project. Throughout the process, I spent a lot of time studying about the spiritual (and also physical) nature of childbirth. I read a handful of books (most I had already read during my last 2 pregnancies). [The Gift of Giving Life](https://www.amazon.com/Gift-Giving-Life-Rediscovering-Pregnancy/dp/0615622526/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1\&keywords=the+gift+of+giving+life\&qid=1622403515\&sr=8-1) [The Sacred Gift of Childbirth](https://www.amazon.com/Sacred-Gift-Childbirth-Empowered-Choices-ebook/dp/B01EMGTSLE/ref=sr_1_5?dchild=1\&keywords=sacred+childbirth\&qid=1622403538\&sr=8-5) [Ina May's Guide to Childbirth](https://www.amazon.com/Ina-Mays-Guide-Childbirth-Gaskin/dp/0553381156/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1\&keywords=ina+may%27s+guide+to+childbirth\&qid=1622403582\&sr=8-1) [Back Labor No More](https://www.amazon.com/Back-Labor-No-More-Should/dp/092621800X/ref=sr_1_1?dchild=1\&keywords=back+labor+no+more\&qid=1622403612\&sr=8-1) I searched the scriptures and several resources on the church website. I went back through my journal entries, my prayer journal entries and also re-read the priesthood blessings that Jershon had given me throughout my pregnancy. I also had many experiences where I wrote the words that I felt the Lord was speaking to me right in that moment. Many of these experiences were emotional for me as the veil was parted a bit here and there and I felt the presence of my Heavenly Parents. In the middle of March, I had the idea come to me to gather a few pieces of artwork that really spoke to me in regards to childbirth and take them with me to the hospital to have as a visual focus. I had a couple in mind, but I decided to post about it on the Well-Educated Heart facebook page to see if anyone else had any others that they would recommend. Several women responded and gave me some other paintings to look at as options but none of them really stood out to me. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0957.jpeg) These are the paintings that I settled on: Top: Holding Hands (I searched everywhere and couldn't find the artist), [She will Find What is Lost by Brian Kershisnik](https://www.heirloomartco.com/products/brian-kershisnik-she-will-find-what-is-lost-art#:~:text=Print%20%2D%205%20x%207%20Card,actually%20surrounded%20by%20heavenly%20help.), [Peace in Christ by Kate Lee](https://deseretbook.com/p/peace-in-christ-print-kate-lee?queryID=a6fce234f1ecaf80f8a5eae5c9132e65\&variant_id=184056-5x7-print) Bottom: Christ Hugging Woman (I searched everywhere and couldn't find the artist), [Mother to Mother by Katie Garner](https://ldsart.com/products/mother-to-mother-by-katie-garner?variant=35307837358233), [The Hand of God by Yongsung Kim ](https://ldsart.com/collections/yongsung-kim/products/the-hand-of-god-by-yongsung-kim?variant=35324450537625)One girl commented and told me about a [Joyful and Spiritual Birth](http://joyfulandspiritualbirth.com/) class (geared towards members of the church) that I might be interested in. I looked into it and decided that it looked really interesting (and it was free too which was a bonus) so I decided to start working my way through the materials which consisted of a 100+ page workbook and also some spiritual pregnancy/birth meditations that go along with the reading. It was so neat to be able to read through all of this material, and also to listen to the meditations. I kept having so many validating moments as I read through everything because nearly all of the points that were discussed where things that I had felt impressed to include in my own labor/birth meditation script which I had been writing for 2 months by that point. It was also neat to listen to the meditations. I'm grateful that I was able to be introduced to these resources... but I'm also grateful that I didn't know that this existed until _after_ I had already done so much towards writing my own meditation. If I had found this resource at the very beginning when I had done that initial google search, I probably would have missed out on several really personal and special experiences that I had while writing and feeling very led by God to know what to write. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_9348.jpeg) 27 weeks The thing is, I just kept writing, and writing, and writing (and gathering more things to include in my meditation script as I studied about childbirth), and I had no idea how long the audio would end up being after I recorded it, and also I had no idea on how to know when I was finished or not. Along the way, I was trying to find a song to put in the background that would both be long enough to accommodate the entire meditation (which remember, I had no idea how long it would actually end up being), and also that spoke to my soul and touched me deeply. I spent many hours searching for a good meditation song that both spoke to me, but wasn't super expensive. I had finally found one that fit these requirements. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0534.jpeg) 33 weeks When I was about a month out from my due date, I decided that I really needed to get to the point where I could be finished writing so I could record the meditation. The kids and Jershon planned to watch a movie in the basement one night so I figured this would be perfect time to shut myself in my closet upstairs and record the meditation. By that afternoon, I still didn't know if the script was all the way complete or not (I didn't really know how to finish it) but I decided that I could just add on more at the end in another recording session if I needed to. I had a little over 18 pages written. That evening, I sat down and just started recording. I had to stop every once in a while to re-record certain sections that I messed up on while talking, but all in all, the process went pretty smoothly. As I got to the end, I found that the text that I had written was 2 minutes longer (only because of all the pausing that I had done throughout the meditation script) than the song. I was able to fix that though by copying a little bit of the song and inserting it in a spot where it wouldn't be noticed very easily that it was inserted. This felt like a miracle because it felt like Heavenly Father was showing me that the script was, in fact, complete. It matched the song nearly perfectly in length...which was miraculous since I had had no idea how long the script was going to be when I said it out loud and added in moments where it was only the music playing and no words being spoken. So I finished it up the next morning, just fixing a few things here and there on the recording program to help the sound quality. And then I exported it as an mp3, transferred it to my phone, and called it good. It ended up being about 1 hour long. I felt so good after finishing it. I felt like, with God, I had accomplished a monumental task and that He had led me every step of the way. I looked forward to listening to this meditation script throughout my labor and delivery which was a few weeks away. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0880-2.jpeg) 36 weeks ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0895.jpeg) ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0911.jpeg) ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_0940.jpeg) Another thing that I felt impressed to do was a 40 day social media fast, leading up to the birth. 40 is a significant and symbolic number. The gestation period for growing a baby is 40 weeks. 40 symbolizes a period of testing, trial, or probation. It can also represent a generation of man. Forty years can represent another kind of rebirth - the rebirth of an entire generation. The number forty is connected to the concept of creation. The number 40 signifies new life, new growth, transformation, a change from one great task to another great task. Forty brings to mind such qualities as repentance, newness, preparation, self-examination, transformation, task fulfillment, escape from bondage or slavery, nourishment and growth, and a new generation and new life. Since I didn't know when I would actually go into labor, I decided to start my fast 50 days before the birth, just in case (because these things are important to me. haha). This fast was sooo good for me. It was really nice to step away from the mind, messages, and distractions of the world. I was able to really focus on preparing my mind, body, heart, and spirit for this birth. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1008.jpeg) 37 weeks Along with writing the spiritual birth meditation script and doing the 40 day social media fast, I wanted to be as prepared as possible with practical labor and birth knowledge as well. And although this was my 5th pregnancy and birth, I can always learn more and this time I feel like I learned so much more than I knew before. My favorite resource (in addition to the books that I mentioned earlier) was the [Built to Birth](https://builttobirth.com/) course by Bridget Teyler. I learned so much from Bridget and now that my birth experience has passed, I can see how I was able to use this knowledge to my benefit in many ways. Bridget also has a ton of information on her [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCsqMRhkuuI5aiJAVxgUpgbQ) as well. Nearly every afternoon, for about the last couple months of my pregnancy, I would sit on my birth ball and lean over the rocking chair with a bunch of pillows piled up on it. I would listen to some of the pregnancy and birth meditations (from Joyful and Spiritual Birth and the Built to Birth courses)...and often fall asleep. After waking up from my nap, I would turn on some motherhood music and just try to connect with my baby. Remember how I mentioned in my last post that one of my fears surrounding bringing another baby into our home was that I already didn't feel like a good enough mom to the 4 kids I already had? Well, Satan knows this. So he used this opportunity to try to get to me, nearly every time. When I would take the time to sit down and try to connect with this baby that was growing within me...and often add music about motherhood (and the ideals that I would like to live up to) to that experience...I had a hard time just focusing on thoughts and feelings of excitement, joy, and bonding. Instead, I would feel Satan flood me with feelings of shame for not being a perfect mother to the children that I already have. I would experience him filling me with thoughts of "who do you think you are to bring another child into this world? You already fail so much with the kids that you already have. You just aren't cut out for this." Now if you are reading this, you probably think I must be some horrible mother in order to have these thoughts. haha. I'm not going to pretend that I'm perfect (far from it!), but I also realize that Satan is full of lies. He doesn't like what I am trying to build here. He doesn't like that I am _trying_ to be a good mother... no matter how many times I make mistakes (which is a lot). He lets me know that frequently by trying to get me to feel like a failure. He tries to make me see my mistakes, weaknesses, and shortcomings magnified times 100 and he tries to extremely minimize any strengths, successes, and positive progress. Sometimes he's pretty successful at getting me down. Anyway... these messages of me being a failure already are the messages that Satan sent to me often _before_ I got pregnant with this baby... he was trying to convince me that I shouldn't have any more children because I wasn't the best mother to the ones I already had and I would be doing my current children _and_ the new child a disservice. ...and I believed him for quite a while... before Heavenly Father's message finally got through to me and I decided to act in faith and trust that God would help me. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1043.jpeg) 38 weeks All in all, I had a really great pregnancy and after getting past the morning sickness phase, I felt pretty good, even up until the end. My iron levels got a little low by my 3rd trimester (not technically anemia levels though), but after taking an iron supplement for a couple months everything returned to normal with that. I did have more pelvic joint pain (I think from the relaxin hormone) than with my other pregnancies, but that seemed to come and go a bit. It seemed to be the most pronounced during my 2nd trimester where I noticed it day and night, but by the end, I didn't notice it much during the day when I was up and moving around, but I would notice it a lot at night when I would roll over or get out of bed. My feet would get pretty sore and more swollen by the end of a long day as well, but besides that, I didn't have much to complain about and I'm grateful for that. It was music to my ears at my prenatal appointments to always hear my midwife say "everything looks perfect!" As my pregnancy progressed towards the birth, I felt really grateful for all of the miracles that I had been experiencing...especially for my good health and that the baby seemed to be healthy as well. ![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1150.jpeg) 38 weeks Throughout my pregnancy I really felt like this baby had been (and would continue to be) a gift from God to me and to our family. This baby has changed me in many ways. This baby had increased my faith and my willingness to do God's will, even when it's not part of my plan. This baby had brought so many miracles into my life (and our family) already. Heavenly Father told me that I would see miracles and that had definitely been the case.
