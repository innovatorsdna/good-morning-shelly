I've written about this before, so if you've been here for a while then this won't be new but I wanted to make a separate post about it because it means so much to me. I never imagined that I would have sheep someday. I don't think it had ever crossed my mind. Not until I started learning about my Swedish heritage at least. When we did our [Swedish Unit](/swedish-unit/), the dream of someday getting a farm and raising sheep began to grow within me. A few months later, we did our [Sheep Unit](/sheep-unit/) and the dream grew even deeper and bigger. And then a few years ago, that dream became a reality and we got our [farm](/theres-no-place-like-home-in-kansas/)! I still can't believe it. So much has changed in the last 5 years. When we were in the middle of our Swedish unit and the sheep dream had started to grow, I remember that I was at a Relief Society activity one evening and we were sharing little tidbits about ourselves. I remember telling the women that someday I wanted to own sheep and I would name my first ewe, Thilda, after my great-great grandmother, [Bothilda Pehrsdotter](/swedish-unit-bothilda-pehrsdotter-and-wool/). Last summer, that ewe, Thilda, joined our farm and I'm so happy about it. We finally have Swen the ram and Thilda the ewe, both named after my great-great grandparents that inspired this whole sheep farm to begin with. :)
