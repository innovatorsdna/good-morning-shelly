Sometimes when you've grown up surrounded by something, you take it for granted and don't really realize what you have. That's how I feel when it comes to the gospel of Jesus Christ. Lately I've been trying to take the time to really reflect on some of these things and think deeply about what they mean for me and how my life has been and continues to be blessed because of them.

Throughout the past couple weeks, I have tried to gather my thoughts and feelings about Easter - Jesus Christ's Atonement, Crucifixion, and Resurrection.

Easter is about celebrating the condescension of God

Because of Easter (Jesus’ atonement and resurrection), we can trust that God loves us and keeps His promises

—prophets for thousands of years prophesied of Christ, who He was, and what He would do for the world

—those prophesies were fulfilled during Easter week

—Jesus overcame death and established Himself as having authority over all things

—we can trust from this that God will continue to keep His promises, including that Christ will come again and reign in peace and glory and that all will be made right

Because of Easter, death is not the end. It’s just a transition to a different state.

—we can be with our families forever

—our life has purpose and meaning and we will continue to progress and grow, even after this life

-Family connections (with the living and the dead) are important and rewarding

Because of Easter, we can overcome the natural man

—we get second (and third, fourth, etc.) chances

-we can repent

-we can be sanctified and strengthened through the Holy Ghost to become more than we could be on our own

-I can be born again

Because of Easter, I don’t have to face this life alone

—Christ experienced everything that I will ever go through, during His time in the garden of gethsemane. And He has such perfect compassion that when I am going through those things, He is right there with me, helping me through them in the way that I need most

—I don’t have to fear

—the atonement isn’t only for my sins. It’s for my weaknesses, fears, trials, magnification when I’m not enough on my own, pains, sicknesses, infirmities

Because of Easter, I know about and have a personal relationship with Jesus Christ

—He knows me personally

—He sends me good gifts

—He knows me and has a work for _me&#x20;_&#x74;o do

Because of Easter, I know that my Heavenly Parents are real and they love me

—For God so loved the world that He gave His only Begotten Son

Because of Easter, I can live with my Heavenly Parents again

—Christ fulfilled the law and if I come unto Him and bind myself to Him, I can be worthy to dwell in the purity of the Celestial Kingdom.

—I could never be good enough or perfect enough on my own

Because of Easter, we can escape from the grasp of Satan

—the monster, death (of the body) and hell (death of the spirit)

—We wouldn’t have just completely disappeared to no existence without the resurrection of the body... our spirits would have become like Satan ad angels to the devil (2nd Nephi 9:8-9)

Because of Easter, we can be made whole

-the resurrection doesn’t only restore limbs, joints, hair, and physical health. It also restores mental health. And although I don’t suffer from clinical or severe mental health problems, I think that I do have mild (or maybe just normal for mortality) anxiety and other mental health challenges. Anything that is broken in me, that was caused by mortal decay, or by the choices of others (like how i was raised, any childhood wounding that occurred, unintentionally for the most part) will be made whole again. Having my mind and emotional state made whole is exciting and something i am looking forward to

Because of Easter, I don’t have to carry the weight of the world (or of my own world).

Because of Easter, I can have new life -any day, hour, minute, or second that I choose to.

Friday (the cross) looked like failure. But Sunday (the resurrection) shows the victory.

Fig tree - Christ has the power to make things die as well.

Because of Easter, I know that Jesus is the Christ

[https://www.​churchofjesuschrist.org/study/​ensign/2018/12/i-am-that-i-am-​symbols-of-jesus-christ-in-​the-old-testament?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/ensign/2018/12/i-am-that-i-am-symbols-of-jesus-christ-in-the-old-testament?lang=eng)

Experiencing things through our imagination is experiencing it with our spirits. But the physical experience with our bodies is even greater... although sometimes not as good because of our thoughts... but with a resurrected body, our minds will be perfected as well. The experiences that we have will be incredible and couldn’t be as fulfilling without a body.

-imagining a warm shower is nice, but actually physically experiencing a warm shower is way better. The only thing that gets in the way of this is our thoughts (and other physical sensations because of injury or something.) but with a resurrected body we won’t have these problems
