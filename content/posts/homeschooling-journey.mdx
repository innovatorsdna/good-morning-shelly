August 30, 2018 I thought I would take some time to just journal and do some introspection about why I _do_ want to do homeschool and why I am feeling drawn to it. First, I know that homeschooling will not be rainbows and unicorns all the time. I know that it will be hard. I know that it will be stressful at times and I will doubt myself at times. But I also know that Heavenly Father would be with me every step of the way. I want to homeschool because I want to be able to spend more time with my kids and be able to teach them more. It's only 2 weeks into the school year, but I am noticing more this time around (because of our home summer school experience) that I really don't have much time with the kids during the day. There are 24 hours in the day. They are asleep for 11 of those hours. And they are at school for about 7 hours. That leaves us with 6 hours left in the day. When you consider that 2 of those hours are meal times, another couple hours are used up in just getting ready for the day and getting ready for bed, we are now down to 2 hours left. Then you throw in that kids should be kids and they should have time to just play. And also moms need to run the household and cook meals and clean and such, it really becomes a major challenge to spend any good quantity of time being able to just sit and teach and learn together. I figure that on a _good_ day, I have about 1-2 hours per day (not all together... it's split up into 15-30 minute chunks throughout the entire 6 hours that the kids are home and awake) to spend with the kids and be their teacher. And that is _with_ me being pretty intentional and sticking with the schedule that I made. When I'm not being intentional, that amount of time is definitely decreased. Or on the days that they want to have a friend over, or just want to spend more time playing (and I feel like they should do those things because they have already been at school all day,) then I have pretty much no time at all with them. Is that the way it's supposed to be? Aren't parents supposed to be the greatest influence in their child's life ... at least until they are quite a bit older? Then why do the parents spend the _least_ amount of time with the child (during his/her waking hours)? The crazy thing is that I probably spend more time with my kids at home than most people. A lot of people have their kids in extracurricular activities - _outside_ of school... so that is _even_ more time that the child is not at home or with his/her parents. Also, the norm these days is to send your kids to preschool - starting when they are about 3 years old. I've always been "the weird one" that doesn't put my kids in preschool. It seems like having our kids "leave the nest" (even if it's just for a chunk of the day and not for real like moving out of the house) is happening at an earlier and earlier age. Especially when you consider that a lot of moms work and send their _babies!!&#x20;_&#x6F;ff to daycare for a large chunk (pretty much like going to school all day) of the day. Being able to spend more time with my kids and be able to be their biggest influence at this stage of their lives is a big reason why I am drawn to homeschooling. I also feel that homeschooling would draw us closer together as a family and to Jesus Christ. Because we don't see each other very much during the day, it is sooooo easy for my relationship to get strained with Landon and Porter. I have felt it every year since Landon started first grade (and has been gone "all day" at school.) It just occurred to me that I have been thinking all this time that the boys _need_ to be at school because they _need_ to socialize and gain confidence. They _need_ to come out of their shells. But perhaps it's just the opposite? Perhaps what the _need_ is to be at home. With me. They _need_ more of my time and attention. They _need_ to know and feel that they are loved unconditionally. They need me to be there, cheering them on and encouraging them and facilitating learning opportunities. That will give them more true confidence than anything. For me, I have gained the most confidence since being married. Sure, some of that has come from me doing hard things. But I think that most of it has a lot to do with having someone that knows me so well - even all of my flaws and weaknesses, and _still_ loves me. It comes from having that person that believes in me and cheers me on. I feel like this is similar to what would happen if our kids were home and we had more time together... if I really _knew_ our kids, and they truly felt _loved_. I also know that they would have a better opportunity to come to know Heavenly Father better and that they are His child. And THAT is what will give them more true confidence than anything. I also feel that stepping out into the unknown like this and taking on this big responsibility to be my children's sole and official teacher would push me to greater heights in spirituality. I would _have_ to rely on the Savior and spirit more in my life. September 11, 2018 Well, I did it. I talked to Jershon about homeschooling. Last night as we were getting ready for bed, I just felt so nervous. I didn't know if it was time yet or not but I just couldn't stop thinking about it. I prayed and asked Heavenly Father to help me to know if I should bring it up and what I should say. Then as we were laying in bed, I just kept feeling like I should tell him, so I did. I started by saying "there is something that I would like you to think and pray about." Then I told him that homeschooling has been on my heart and mind a lot over the past few weeks. I told him that I haven't come to a complete conclusion myself yet, but I have been researching it. I told him that I want him to have his own process with it and that if we were to decide to do this, we would both need to be on board. Then I briefly gave him some of my reasons, including the example of him trying to work on a really important project but only having a small amount of time each day and comparing that to the monumental task that I have has a mother to teach and prepare our kids for life. I also told him that when I think about the reasons _not_ to homeschool, they are fear based. He was pretty quiet... which is what I expected. I know that he has doubts and reservations and it's gonna take him some time to have his heart softened (if that's Heavenly Father's will) towards the idea. But at least it didn't turn into a fight or anything. He wasn't outright negative about the idea - just didn't really say anything. Coincidentally, as we were getting ready for bed (before I brought up the homeschooling topic), I was mentioning that tomorrow night (tonight) was my first online book group meeting with my friend, Jenny (who I met from Instagram). We are reading and discussing a book called "Let God Love You : Why we don't, how we can." I read Jershon a quote that had stuck out to me from my reading today... "It is difficult to grow a satisfying, trusting relationship with Divinity when prior relationship losses, disappointments, and fears entangle us in false hopes and impossible expectations. Why do our Father and Savior seem to stay so far away? How can we learn to approach these - our most important relationships - with less worry and shame, more trust and hope? How can we more fully receive the love that we've been told They offer but that we don't always feel? Without fully realizing it, many of us fear the vulnerability inherent in such closeness. We may yearn for an intimacy with God that we believe would quiet our fears, strengthen our confidence, and reassure our faith. But we are afraid of intimacy when we realize it would also expose our inadequacies or make us vulnerable to loss. We may have learned in relationships to feel overwhelmed, used, ignored, or afraid of betrayal, rejection, or disappointment. We may explicitly or implicitly worry that God will also criticize, ignore, deplete, or test us beyond our ability to endure." I mentioned that I think this is interesting and I can see it in my own life. There are times (probably more often than not) that I really would like to have a close relationship with Heavenly Father, but I have this fear (subconscious usually) that if I get to close, then He will ask me to do something that is really hard and uncomfortable. Of course, even if I'm _not_ that close to Him, He could still put a trial into my life and/or ask me to do something hard... but if I _am_ close to Him, then I will better recognize the promptings and then feel more pressure and guilt to actually _follow_ the prompting and _do_ the hard thing. So strangely, I think that I distance myself from God because getting too close means facing hard and uncomfortable things. I think that I have been experiencing some of this with the homeschooling idea. I have been _praying_ about it (but sort of half way), and I _think_ that I am willing to do it if that's what Heavenly Father wants me to do, but at the same time, I am absolutely terrified. I am terrified of the responsibility that will be placed on my shoulders. Sending my kids to public school is easy. I don't have to do any of the work. I just drop them off, hope they learn something, pick them up, and then hope that they get into college someday. Homeschooling puts all of that responsibility on me. And that is terrifying. I don't know everything. There is a lot that I don't know. But yet, I would be their teacher. I have a fear of "messing my kids up" - especially socially. I fear that I'm not enough for them in this category. I fear that my driving insecurities and introverted personality will hold them back from getting to interact with people. I fear being "the weird family". I fear not being able to measure up to the task. But at the same time, I feel the spirit whispering to me that it will be ok. That whom the Lord calls, He qualifies. That I _am_ qualified. He sent these specific kids to _me_! He knows them personally and individually and He knows _me_ personally and individually. He knows what we need and He will not abandon or forget us. He knows our futures and knows how to prepare us for those futures. I feel the spirit telling me that homeschooling will force (well, not _force_, but push at least) me to strengthen my relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ. I will _have_ to lean on them for help, support, knowledge, etc. They will become my co-teachers. They will help me to teach our kids. They will help me when I feel like giving up and when I am facing my fears head on. I also feel the spirit telling me that homeschooling will help my kids to develop a stronger relationship with God as well. That their education can be filled with Christ. I also feel the spirit telling me that homeschooling will bring me more joy. That motherhood will have more meaning and purpose and fulfillment.
