Something that I have been trying out in this new year is focusing my morning routine on my 2018 word. The word that I am choosing to focus on this year is _Feel_. If you're interested in why I chose that word, you can read about it [here](/2018/01/01/2018-word-of-the-year-feel/). Here are just a few things that I've come up with and have been practicing so far to focus my routine on my 2018 word: I don't love to exercise. Out of all of the S.A.V.E.R.S. it's the one that I struggle with the most. Its something that I know is important though and I'd like to get better at. I was thinking the other day, that I could use exercise as a way to work on allowing and leaning into my _feelings_ - both positive and negative. What I've started doing is working out to dance exercise videos on YouTube. I am not a dancer. Far from it. Haha. I feel super vulnerable and awkward when I dance. So this has been good practice in vulnerability - even though I'm just by myself. :) I have also been trying to really _feel_ the music and allow myself to _feel_ happy. It's been a good practice in letting go and just _feeling_. It's also been a good way to get some exercise at the same time. :) My personal growth reading has also been focused on learning more about emotions and how to process them and learn from them. My affirmations have been focused on allowing and leaning into my feelings. In my prayers, I've been asking for additional guidance and help as I learn how to feel more deeply and how to act more consciously rather than just reacting and letting my emotions control me. My meditation practice is helping me to learn to be more present and how to calm myself when I'm worked up. Both of these skills are important in the process of allowing and leaning into positive and negative emotions. I've been journaling about my successes and attempts and failures with this process of learning how to open my heart and how to feel. I know that I'm not very far into 2018 yet, but I am excited to see what kind of progress I make this year as I intentionally put my focus on learning how to really feel and I know that my morning routine is going to be a big part of that progress. -3 things I'm grateful for...really feeling the gratitude
