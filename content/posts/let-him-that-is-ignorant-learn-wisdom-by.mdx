"Let him that is ignorant learn wisdom by humbling himself and calling upon the Lord his God, that his eyes may be opened that he may see, and his ears opened that he may hear;" (Doctrine and Covenants 136:32) "They have mouths, but they speak not: eyes have they, but they see not: They have ears, but they hear not: noses have they, but they smell not: They have hands, but they handle not: feet have they, but they walk not: neither speak they through their throat." (Psalm 115:5-7) Interestingly, these verses are referring to the idols (false gods) that were made by the hands of the people. These false gods didn't have mouths that could _actually_ speak, eyes that could _actually_ see, ears that could _actually_ hear, noses that could _actually_ smell, hands that could _actually_ handle and touch, feet that could _actually_ walk. The people were worshiping and placing their trust on a mirage of a god - a god that wasn't even a god at all. It actually had _no_ power to do anything for them. It had no ability to create or to make anything happen. But worshiping something that is right in front of you is probably easier than worshiping _Someone_ that can't be seen (or more accurately, isn't _usually_ seen) with our physical eyes. How often do we make our physical senses into false gods - trusting only in what we see right in front of us - instead of seeing with the eye of faith and seeing what is possible with the one and only _real_ God? How often do we rely so heavily on our physical view of the world and forget to see the world and its possibilities through our spiritual senses? It feels more vulnerable. It feels less certain. It feels less tangible. "And these are the words which he spake and caused to be written, saying: My brethren, all ye that have assembled yourselves together, you that can hear my words which I shall speak unto you this day; for I have not commanded you to come up hither to trifle with the words which I shall speak, but that you should hearken unto me, and open your ears that ye may hear, and your hearts that ye may understand, and your minds that the mysteries of God may be unfolded to your view." (Mosiah 2:9)
