Today was Easter. This morning I woke up at 6, like normal (except sometimes I sleep a little later on the weekends). No one else was awake yet so I went down to the kitchen and living room and set up for our sunrise devotional. I wanted to make it more comfortable this year so I moved the table and chairs (like I have done a million times before when I turn our dining room into something else) and I brought up the couch cushions from the basement to make a couch on the floor, facing the sliding glass door so we could watch the sunrise. ![](/uploads/2020/04/IMG_8750-1.jpg) Jesus' tomb was in a garden, right? So it seemed fitting that we were sitting in our garden, waiting for the sun to rise.

![](/uploads/2020/04/IMG_8745-1.jpg)

I woke everyone else up around 6:30 and we came down to the kitchen. We talked about the empty tomb and Jesus's resurrection. We read the story in the Bible. We talked about some of our favorite scripture stories and scripture verses about Jesus. And we sang the hymn, He is Risen. Then we sat there, listening to instrumental hymns as we waited for the sun to rise above the mountain (a process that took longer than I anticipated). The kids were pretty good up until this point, but then they started getting grumpy and irritated with each other. And I got frustrated with them. And I was frustrated with myself for being frustrated with them. I just wanted this to be special moment. Sometimes I feel so frustrated that my spiritual moments that I try to create and invite in are ruined because the kids are...being kids. And I just feel stuck in this predicament of either just giving up, throwing in the towel, and just not expecting to have these special, sacred moments until my kids are older (or maybe even out of the house), or just don't invite them in the first place and have these moments all on my own. But the thing is... I don't want to have to wait 15 more years. And I _do_ want them to be a part of these experiences. And they want to be a part of them too. Anyway... after some frustration, and some silent prayer, I had a moment of clarity and I told everyone that we were going to end our devotional with a prayer and then go eat breakfast. I said the prayer and I felt a lot better. I thanked Heavenly Father for Jesus and that because of Him, we can have fresh starts and new beginnings, any moment that we choose to. And I realized that even though I wasn't perfectly loving and patient this morning, Easter (and what it means) is even more precious to me _because_ I make mistakes. I mess up all. the. time. But because of Jesus, I can repent and I can try again. I get an endless amount of second chances. I am extremely grateful for that. This was our 5th Sunday having church at home (church is cancelled until further notice because of Covid-19.) Last week was General Conference so today was our 4th home Sacrament meeting. We have been having Sacrament meeting in the Schoolroom every week but because it was Easter Sunday, I wanted to set up the room a little extra special. I took the books off the shelves that are on the wall right behind the table and put pictures of Christ (that I gathered from the other rooms in our house) on the shelf instead. I love that our schoolroom has also become our sacred sanctuary as well.

Song: Jesus, the Very Thought of Thee played by Paul Cardall (used with permission)

I played the piano (like I've been doing each week). The opening hymn was He is Risen, the Sacrament hymn was I Stand All Amazed, and the closing song was I Know That My Redeemer Lives. Every week, for the white cloth to cover the Sacrament, we have used the white blanket that all 4 of the kids were wrapped in on their blessing days. It is extra special because is from Bethlehem.

![](/uploads/2020/04/IMG_8778-1.jpg)

When I worked at the BYU Law School, my co-worker, Debbi, gave it to me. Her daughter had brought it back from Bethlehem when she was there on a study abroad. I wrote more about Debbi in my entry on January 18th, 2020. When she gave me the blanket, she told me that she had told her daughter to bring a few blankets back so she could give them to her other daughters, and to a few other special people. I was grateful (and a bit surprised) to see that she considered me to be one of those special people. I think she gave it to me when I was pregnant with Landon. ![](/uploads/2020/04/A1C3A0A9-2EC0-4BE6-AC54-6B8EAFD510C0.jpg) I didn't get a good photo of Landon with the blanket. He's crying in all of the photos that has the blanket in it. haha. For the bread, we used some of the leftover Matzah from our [Passover](/passover-2020/) meal. It was a special and unique experience to get to use this as the bread for the Sacrament for a few reasons...

![](/uploads/2020/04/IMG_8833.jpg)

This is the kind of bread (unleavened bread) that Jesus used when He instituted the Sacrament (at the Last Supper). Jershon blessed the bread and brought it around to us. Because there are only 6 of us, the pieces were larger than we typically have at church. And because we used Matzah, which takes longer to chew (its not soft like leavened bread), I had longer to really think and ponder _while_ I was partaking of the bread, which made a difference, I think. I thought about the Sacrament prayer.

> "O God, the Eternal Father, we ask thee in the name of thy Son, Jesus Christ, to bless and sanctify this bread to the souls of all those who partake of it, **that they may eat in remembrance of the body of thy Son**, and witness unto thee, O God, the Eternal Father, that they are willing to take upon them the name of thy Son, and always remember him and keep his commandments which he has given them; that they may always have his Spirit to be with them. Amen." ([Doctrine & Covenants 20:77](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20.77,79?lang=eng\&clang=eng#p76))

As I was partaking of the bread, I had more time to remember Jesus's body _as_ I was eating it. I actually really appreciated that more than I thought I would (I had never even thought about it before.) As I was eating it, I also thought at lot about the symbols that we learned about during our Passover meal. I thought about how Jesus's body was bruised, broken, and torn for me (and how we tore the Matzah in half and hid the afikomen.) I thought about how He takes upon Himself my bitterness, my sins, my weaknesses, my fears, my pains, my sicknesses, my sorrows, my griefs, etc. He carries these things for me (if I give them to Him). I thought about how He takes me out of bondage and into a life of freedom.

![](/uploads/2020/04/IMG_8834.jpg)

As Jershon blessed the water and brought it around to us, I thought about the cup of the covenant and how I am bound to Christ and He is bound to me. We are in a covenant relationship. I thought about how He continually helps me and how I show more love to Him. I thought about how my side of the covenant is to always remember Him, and His side of the covenant is to send His Spirit to be with me.

> "O God, the Eternal Father, we ask thee in the name of thy Son, Jesus Christ, to bless and sanctify this water to the souls of all those who drink of it, that they may do it in remembrance of the blood of thy Son, which was shed for them; that they may witness unto thee, O God, the Eternal Father, that they do always remember him, that they may have his Spirit to be with them. Amen." ([Doctrine & Covenants 20:79](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20.77,79?lang=eng\&clang=eng#p76))

This might have been the most meaningful experience that I've ever had with the actual experience of partaking of the Sacrament before. I really appreciated having this experience, especially on Easter Sunday.
