Life Lessons from Gardening

-plants (especially tomatoes and others like them) need something to hold onto that will keep them strong and upright when the winds blow.

—-we need to hold to the iron rod and if we do, we will make it safely through any storm

\*\* Bethany's painting showing the shepherd staff as the rod that we hold onto

-the soil needs a good supply of the right nutrients.

-hardening off before putting plants out into the big wide uncertain world is important for their future survival and ability to thrive. Putting them out too quickly or too soon has damaging results that are not easy to recover from.

-consistent watering is very important

-daily watch care is essential to catch problems early (like bug infestations, nutrient deficiencies, lack of water, flowers that need to be pollinated, etc.)

—-we need daily watch care as well. We need to be very observant and watchful of ourselves and those we have stewardship over.

-when you’ve done all you can and it still feels like things are outside of your control, pray. God loves to breathe life back into things (and especially people) and to help them thrive again.
