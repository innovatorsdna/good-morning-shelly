hThis is post number 4 in the series about pregnancy and birth. Start with [post #1](/a-baby-waits-faith-precedes-the-miracle/) if you haven't read the beginning of the story yet. I spent the last few months in deep study in regards to learning about pregnancy, labor, birth, and postpartum... more study than I had ever done before during any of my previous pregnancies... which may seem a little funny since this was my 5th baby (and 4th unmedicated birth). It seems like I should feel less of a need to study and learn everything rather than more. ... but instead I felt even more of a desire to dive deep into the learning. This was my first time really doing much study about the postpartum phase though and I learned some really interesting things.

![](/uploads/2021/05/91C4E44A-8895-4F87-9E70-74B70E838AF9.jpg)

I learned about the birth of me as a new mother and how vital the postpartum period is - especially the first 40 days. As I prepared for the birth and the postpartum phase that would come after, I decided that I would do another 40 day social media fast (so really just an extension of my prenatal fast) for the first 40 days postpartum. Again, I felt that this would be a good way to step away from the distractions, messages, and mind of the world. It's been a nice break and has helped me to really focus on my baby, bond with him, and establish a good breastfeeding relationship without being distracted every time I sit down to nurse. I have been trying to be really intentional this time with taking time to just rest and enjoy my baby. This is a special time.

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_5641.jpg)

When a baby is born, so is a mother. Matrescence is the transition to motherhood. Matrescence is similar to adolescence in that it’s a time of major transition, growth, and change – physically, mentally, emotionally, and spiritually.

> "All over the world and in many indigenous cultures, new mothers have been afforded a period of rest and recovery following birth ...
>
> ‘The Golden Month’, ‘The Sitting Moon’, ‘The First Forty Days’. The postpartum weeks have been viewed as an important and potent time where a woman is given time and space to recover from her efforts, fall in love with her new baby and learn to be the mother she wants to be. During this time she is nurtured and nourished by her community who gather around to support, feed and celebrate her. It is understood that this will have lifelong benefits for both mother and baby going forward.
>
> More recently, modern-day neuroscience confirms that during pregnancy, birth and postpartum, a woman’s brain is literally transfigured during a period of plasticity that has been labelled ‘matrescence.’" ([Jojo Hogan](https://slowpostpartum.com/blog/three-reasons-to-choose-a-slow-postpartum-with-your-new-baby))

> "Pregnancy and birth are a time of brain re-wiring. There are very few times in our lives when we have an opportunity for this exciting change. Due to the changes that are occurring, a woman’s brain goes through a stage of ‘plasticity’, which means that it is able to re-wire and change. Each time a woman gives birth is an amazing opportunity for positive growth – for both herself and her family. You’ve heard of ‘baby brain’? This is a real thing, and something that you should own proudly! It’s part of this process of brain re-wiring and occurs as the brain shuffles things around in order for a brain that is better designed for motherhood. Sure, some things may not be as they were before, but other parts are enhanced. Pretty cool huh? " ([Briony Goodsell](https://www.thewomenspic.com/new-blog/2020/1/20/matrescence-and-the-birth-of-a-mother))

> "‘Baby Brain’ is a real thing and although it is derided and dismissed in our culture, recent neuroscience shows the changes to a woman’s brain in the last few weeks of pregnancy and the first few weeks postpartum are actually incredibly important and designed to prepare her for effectively bonding with, protecting and breastfeeding her baby. It’s a time of her life (along with puberty and menopause) when the hormonal impact creates the opportunity for brain plasticity – allowing her to learn new skills, to be more emotionally empathetic and aware and to bond, not only with her baby but with others around her at the time. Amazing!!" ([Jojo Hogan](https://www.ecobeautyeditor.com/people/postnatal-care-jo-hogan/))

(more on this [here](https://wellbeingformothers.com/2018/11/21/40-days-for-40-years-why-postpartum-recovery-matters-and-how-to-facilitate-it/) , [here](https://www.birthtrauma.org.au/what-is-matrescence/), [here](https://slowpostpartum.com/blog/why-baby-brain-is-a-blessing), [here](https://slowpostpartum.com/blog/bouncing-back-after-birth-and-other-bs), [here](https://wellbeingformothers.com/2018/10/27/becoming-a-mother-is-a-process-that-has-been-given-a-name-its-called-matrescence/), and [here](https://wellbeingformothers.com/2018/10/03/the-wonderful-thing-that-is-baby-brain/))

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1448.jpg)

Matrescence, especially the first 40 days is a period of rest.

> "The natural rhythm of the Earth and Life—is that there is a period of growth and a period of rest. This is how balance is created. Pregnancy represents the time of growth, and therefore the postpartum period represents the time of rest. In the modern world, there is no value given for rest—modern culture is focused on constant growth. To reset in the postpartum time, is to be in harmony with the natural rhythms of Life. To be in harmony with the natural rhythms of Life is our path to health."—Rachelle Garcia Seliga, CPM ([Sage Birth and Wellness Collective](http://sagebirthandwellness.com/2018/04/postpartum-rest-40-days-laying/))

I've been in the thick of matrescence for the past 11 1/2 years, but as I am currently 3 weeks postpartum, I'm presently experiencing the stage of brain plasticity and rewiring.

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1499.jpg)

This postpartum period has been different than my past 4 births. It's had some extra challenges because of the blood pressure thing. It's weird because all in all, I have felt great. I have energy (even though I get woken up multiple times a night), my bleeding is hardly anything at all anymore, my back isn't sore anymore (it was kind of sore the first few days after birth), my perineum feels pretty normal again (I'm not sure if the stitches have dissolved yet or not or if my tear is all the way healed yet, but if it isn't, I don't feel it anymore), I'm not having anymore after birth cramping/contractions (and haven't since a few days postpartum). My belly doesn't even feel extra giggly or anything (even though I definitely still have weight to lose and tightening up to do). Crosby's latch is great so my nipples aren't even sore and now that my milk supply is stabilizing, I don't even feel extra engorged or anything most of the time. Pretty much my only noticeable postpartum symptom is that my tailbone still gets sore when I sit for too long. ... so on the outside, I feel really good. ... but yet because of the blood pressure drama, I felt like a ticking time bomb for some reason. ... like I could be completely fine and either the blood pressure thing was just anxiety induced to begin with, or it was just a temporary thing that would completely resolve soon... or something is chronically wrong and I'll need to get medical assistance.

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1667.jpg)

A little over a week after giving birth, my midwife wanted me to come in for a follow up appointment, mostly just to check on my blood pressure since it had been all over the place (with some higher readings) at the hospital. I had been monitoring it at home a little bit over the past week but it was causing me too much stress and stripping the new baby joy (plus, deep down I felt that it was more due to blood pressure cuff anxiety than something else) so I hadn't monitored it consistently. When I got to my appointment, my blood pressure was a little high...which was frustrating (but again, I felt like it was just anxiety). It wasn't high enough for my midwife to feel like something needed to be done immediately (and not even high enough that they would consider putting me on medication) but she told me that she wanted me to monitor it at home regularly over the next week and then talk to her on the phone the next Friday. If it was still consistently high then I might need to talk to a cardiologist. On the drive home, I was just trying to hold back the tears. I was emotionally exhausted from the stress of not knowing if I could trust my own feelings of it just being anxiety induced or if there was actually something wrong that needed medical attention. Of course I didn't want to ignore it if there really was something wrong. But I also didn't want to medically intervene if nothing was chronically wrong. When she mentioned the cardiologist thing, I was reminded of a conversation that I had with Heavenly Father in my prayer journal a couple months ago. He told me that this baby and I were best friends and that we would recognize that connection as time goes on. He also told me that a year ago during General Conference, I gave Him my heart and this baby has been sent to heal me, to teach me, and to give me a new heart. When I remembered this and connected it with the cardiologist comment, I felt some fear and wondered if there was something wrong with my physical heart that would need to be healed and made new.

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1787-2.jpg)

When I got home from the appointment, I just felt raw and stressed and needed to talk to God about all of it. I sat down and had a conversation with Him in my prayer journal. Through this, He told me that I didn't need to worry and that my blood pressure would normalize (I didn't know when though) ...but that this is an opportunity for me to exercise my faith, to look forward with an eye of faith, to look to Him and live. He told me to turn my heart over to Him and allow this experience to make me into a more calm, sensitive, and peaceful mother and person. He told me that my blood pressure is sensitive and is best maintained with a calm heart and to breathe deeply and regularly. When I am feeling upset, take a few moments to breathe and then respond instead of react and by small and simple things are great things brought to pass. These experiences will help to give me a new heart and will change me as a mother and as a person.

> "Ye cannot behold with your natural eyes, for the present time, the design of your God concerning those things which shall come hereafter, and the glory which shall follow after much tribulation. For after much tribulation come the blessings. Wherefore the day cometh that ye shall be crowned with much glory; the hour is not yet, but is nigh at hand. Remember this, which I tell you before, that you may lay it to heart, and receive that which is to follow." ([Doctrine and Covenants 58:3-5](https://abn.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/58?lang=eng))

So that's what I committed to do. I committed to follow the directions of the ultimate cardiologist and heart doctor - Jesus Christ.

So this is what I have been actively trying to work on these past couple weeks. I've been trying to work on being a more calm, peaceful, sensitive, and responsive (instead of reactive) mother. Have I been perfect at it? No. But I don't think Heavenly Father expects perfection. When I mess up, I repent and try again. I have had some successful moments and for that I am grateful. I also made a list on my phone, as a reminder to myself, of the kind of mother that I want to be and certain actions that I want to have in order to practice being that kind of mother. Another thing that I felt impressed to do was to start a gratitude list on my phone where I wrote down things each day that I noticed and was grateful for. This helped me to focus on the blessings in my life and helped me to feel more peace and joy.

> "Verily I say unto you my friends, fear not, let your hearts be comforted; yea, rejoice evermore, and in everything give thanks; Waiting patiently on the Lord, for your prayers have entered into the ears of the Lord of Sabaoth, and are recorded with this seal and testament—the Lord hath sworn and decreed that they shall be granted. Therefore, he giveth this promise unto you, with an immutable covenant that they shall be fulfilled; and all things wherewith you have been afflicted shall work together for your good, and to my name’s glory, saith the Lord." ([D\&C 98: 1-3](https://abn.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/98?lang=eng))

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1820.jpg)

I monitored my blood pressure every morning and evening for the next week. I could feel the anxiety every time I put on that blood pressure cuff and waited for the results to show up on the monitor. But I worked each time to let go of fear and instead focus my faith on Jesus Christ.

> “Hope helps us to walk by faith, not by sight. This can actually be safer. When unaided spiritually, natural sight often shrinks from the odds (see 2 Cor. 5:7). It is immobilized by improbabilities. Mauled by his moods and intimidated by his fears, the natural man overreacts to, while hope overrides, the disappointments of the day.” ([Neal A. Maxwell, Brightness of Hope](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1994/10/brightness-of-hope?lang=eng))

When I first started monitoring it, it was still higher than I would like it to be. Throughout the week, I gradually saw my blood pressure normalize, just like God had told me it would. I am very grateful for this. My midwife called me last Friday to check in and see how things were going and she was happy to hear that my blood pressure was much better. She told me that I could stop monitoring it, unless I felt like I needed to, and that she will just check in with me again at my 6 week appointment in a few weeks. I'll be honest... it feels really vulnerable for me to be sharing all of this story with you when I'm still in the middle of it. I don't know if I'll ever know if this whole blood pressure thing really was something that was diagnosable (gestational hypertension or pre-preeclampsia)... or if it really was white coat syndrome / anxiety. It also may sound to others like I'm making a big deal out of nothing because it really could be much worse and all in all, everything is great. To me, this is real though. To me, this has been a challenge so I'm choosing to acknowledge that. But I am choosing to see this blood pressure thing as a blessing. I am choosing to allow it to show me that my blood pressure is sensitive. My heart is sensitive. It's important that I strive to maintain a calm heart and knowing this is a good motivation to work even more diligently (with a lot of prayer and help from God) at being the kind of mother that I've always wanted (but often felt like I failed at) to be. Again, this is probably making me sound like I'm some terrible mother. I promise, I'm not. All in all, I'm a pretty decent mom. haha. But I do lose my patience more than I would like to. And I do react instead of intentionally and thoughtfully responding more than I would like.

![](/uploads/2021/06/IMG_2447.jpg)

This matrescence thing is awkward, just like adolescence. I'm stumbling through it. But I am being changed into something beautiful in the process.

> Faith, to be faith, must center around something that is not known. Faith, to be faith, must go beyond that for which there is confirming evidence. Faith, to be faith, must go into the unknown. Faith, to be faith, must walk to the edge of the light, and then a few steps into the darkness. If everything has to be known, if everything has to be explained, if everything has to be certified, then there is no need for faith. Indeed, there is no room for it. (Elder Boyd K. Packer, "What is Faith," Faith, Deseret Book Company, 1983, p. 42)

When I was pregnant and Heavenly Father inspired me to write my own Christ centered birth meditation script, and gather/display meaningful heavenly paintings, I thought He was telling me that because it would be helpful for me in my labor and the birth of this baby. And while that was certainly true... I am realizing now that perhaps even more than the birth of the baby, this meditation script (and all the work, study, and experiences that went into writing it), and the heavenly paintings were meant to help me with the birth of myself as a new mother. It's interesting because even though the meditation script and paintings were definitely useful for my labor/birth of this baby... they have been even more useful to me during these past few weeks of the postpartum phase... the birth of me as a new mother phase. I have looked at those paintings a lot as I have sat in the rocking chair nursing Crosby.

![](/uploads/2021/06/IMG_2057.jpeg)

I have rehearsed the words of my meditation script... and replayed the experiences that inspired those words a lot as I have wrestled with the anxiety that this blood pressure drama has caused in me. Both the paintings and the words of the meditation have buoyed me up and helped me to bring myself back to faith, hope, and peace...many, many times as I feel myself sinking into feelings of fear and frustration. I have been trying to see the positive in all of this though. I have been trying to have this blood pressure thing help to propel me forward in growth and improvement as a mom - especially during this postpartum phase where my brain is getting an upgrade and where the filing cabinet of my brain is being dumped out and re-organized.

I have also had the thought that maybe all of these “complications” at the very end of my pregnancy/beginning of postpartum are meant to show me how many miracles I truly did experience during my pregnancy. The contrast shows me how much Heavenly Father was blessing me with miracles during my pregnancy. ...and perhaps this blood pressure thing (and how it is changing me) is a miracle all on it's own.

![](/uploads/2021/06/IMG_2354.jpg)

So here we are, 18 months after I started having strong impressions that perhaps there was another baby that was meant to join our family, and now he's here.

If you remember from my first post in this series (I know\... it's hard to remember something that was like 20,000 words ago...haha) I mentioned the fears that held me back from wanting to bring another baby into this world. Those fears were:

I already felt super inadequate as a mom to the 4 kids that I already had. I also didn't want the big age gap between my kids. I was afraid to go through another pregnancy and birth and that something might go wrong this time. And another somewhat real fear that lingered in the back of my mind as well had to do with my blood pressure.

So how have all of these played out so far? I still feel inadequate as a mom, but I know that this baby was sent to our family for a reason. He is a gift to me and a gift to our family. I'm excited to see how he continues to change us for the better. Satan tried to convince me that bringing another baby into our home would be doing all of us a disservice... but in reality, bringing a baby into our home is proving to be quite the blessing. I don't know how the age gap will play out as the kids get older but as of right now in this very season, it's a blessing. This is the first time (besides when I had my first baby) that I have not had a toddler (or two) to care for at the same time as a newborn. Because of this, I have been able to really rest during this postpartum phase. My other kids are old enough to be pretty independent now (and the olders can help with the youngest if she needs help with anything). I also feel like I've been given a second chance in a way. It's a beautiful thing to get to start this mothering journey over again with a lot more knowledge and wisdom than I had before when my other kids were babies. I have greater vision now for the kind of mother that I want to be and I get to start that fresh with Crosby. I was blessed with many, many miracles throughout my pregnancy and birth(s) (of the baby and myself as a new mother). Even taking the blood pressure thing into consideration, everything went really smoothly with minimal complications and for that I'm super grateful. I've already talked a lot about the blood pressure thing a lot (you're probably tired of it by now. haha) but I'm grateful that my fears didn't hold me back because I'm being changed through it all. Throughout all of this, my brain is being rewired and my heart is being made new. And all of this is possible because I chose to put my faith in God's plan and I chose to turn my heart, mind, and body over to Him. This is a life long journey and process though. I know it's far from over. I trust that there remains an effectual struggle to be made. But with God, anything is possible.
