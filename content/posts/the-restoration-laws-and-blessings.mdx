Today in Sacrament meeting the talks were about Tithing and Fasting. Typically, when talks are given on these subjects, they don't really stand out to me a lot because the speakers usually seem to present the same things that I've already heard a lot before, or more accurately, my heart is more closed off and my ears and eyes are not opened to learn something new. But this time I received greater insight and understanding and I wanted to write it down before I forget... For many centuries, during the great apostasy (and even now among people who are not members of the church), people didn't know about many of God's laws (including the law of tithing and the law of the fast), or they only had a partial or corrupted understanding of the laws. And if they didn't _know_ about these laws, then they couldn't _live_ these laws. And as we know from Doctrine and Covenants, that laws and blessings go together, in a cause and effect sort of way. "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated—And when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law upon which it is predicated." (D\&C 130: 20-21) So if the people couldn't _live_ these laws (the _cause_) then they couldn't obtain the blessings (the _effect_). Often, it is easy to think of the laws/commandments as a burden, as something that we _have_ to do, as just another thing on the list of "do this, don't do this." But laws/commandments are _invitations to&#x20;_&#x6F;btain blessings. It's like Heavenly Father gives us all of the ingredients and if we are willing to do the work, then we get the result. ... Of course, "doing the work" includes the work of allowing Jesus in, to do _His_ work in us, which is the real source of our abilities. But we have to give our will. We have to act. With the restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ came also the restoration of many laws that had been lost or corrupted. And because of these restored laws, we are able to obtain the blessings that are predicated upon those laws. The restoration is still occurring. It wasn't a one time event. It's an ongoing process that continues to move forward. Laws will continue to be restored -laws that are still completely unknown, and also laws that are partially uncovered already, but that will continue to be uncovered until they are completely restored. And as we receive these laws, we are then able to live the laws and obtain the blessings that are predicated upon them. In thinking of the blessings that are connected to the law of the fast, these stood out to me in the talk that was given... "A person who can discipline himself to fast on a regular basis in the way God has designed can resist every temptation, overcome any burden, and become free from any yoke." (Fasting with Power, Shayne M. Bowen, Ensign April 2009) "Now these sons of Mosiah were with Alma at the time the angel first appeared unto him; therefore Alma did rejoice exceedingly to see his brethren; and what added more to his joy, they were still his brethren in the Lord; yea, and they had waxed strong in the knowledge of the truth; for they were men of a sound understanding and they had searched the scriptures diligently, that they might know the word of God. But this is not all; they had given themselves to much prayer, and fasting; therefore they had the spirit of prophecy, and the spirit of revelation, and when they taught, they taught with power and authority of God." (Alma 17:2-3) Living the law of the fast results in blessings of self discipline, resisting temptation, overcoming burdens, becoming free from any yokes, having the spirit of prophesy and revelation, and teaching with power and authority of God. Those are pretty incredible blessings. ... Blessings that wouldn't be attainable if it weren't for the law upon which they are predicated. A talk that goes along really well with all of this is [Abound with Blessings](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2019/04/41renlund?lang=eng) by Elder Dale G. Renlund.
