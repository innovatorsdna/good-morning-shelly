If you know me in real life or follow my [blog](/) and/or [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/goodmorningshelly/) account, then you know that I'm passionate about journal keeping. And if you didn't already know this about me, then by the end of this post you won't have any doubt about where my affections lie. :) Extra credit if I am able to persuade you to fall in love with journaling like I have. August 24th marked 2 years of writing in my journal every single day. I have been a journal keeper (off and on) for about 20 years now but there have definitely been times when I have fallen out of the habit or only written once every couple months or so. But on August 24, 2016, I wrote “I haven’t been very consistent with writing in my journal lately. I really want to change that. I don’t want to look back at these years and regret not recording this piece of my life. I’m gonna set a goal to write a little bit every night. Even if it’s just a couple sentences.” It is really interesting to be able to look back over these past 2 years and not only be glad that I have so many thoughts and feelings and experiences recorded, but also realize that a lot (probably more that I can even know) of the growth that I have experienced over these past couple years has been greatly enhanced by the very act and habit of journaling. I am so passionate about journaling. The benefits are endless.

# What do I journal about?

Journaling is very individual everyone has a unique style. I personally use my journal in variety of ways. **Daily happenings** Of course, I write about the regular things that happen in my day to day life. I write about funny things that my kids said, where we went camping that weekend, who I babysat that day, etc. Sometimes I even write "boring" things like what I made for dinner or how I cleaned the house. I have heard that many people don't keep a journal because they think that their life isn't interesting and worth recording. They think that their posterity would just be bored with reading about their mundane life. I don't think this is true. I think that everyone's life is interesting - way more interesting than we realize - and worth recording. I am going to give you a lot of suggestions of things to record in your journal, but let's just get it out there that future you _and_ your posterity will find a lot of value and interest in learning about what your everyday life was like at the different stages and phases of life. I experienced this first hand when I compiled volume one of my personal history a few years ago. As part of this process, I went through all of my old journal entries. I ran across at least a handful of entries that started out with something like "I don't really know why I am writing this because whoever reads this will probably think it's boring..." and then I would go on to say something mundane that happened that day. Here I was, reading those entries 5+ years later and did I think they were boring? No. I thought they were really interesting. The mundane experiences that I had recorded helped to paint a picture in my mind of what my life was like at that stage. I had forgotten some of those details. And I found it to be really interesting. Ok, now that we have gotten that out of the way, I'll tell you about some other ways that I use my journal to record my life... **Stormy first draft** I learned this concept from Brene Brown, in her book, [Rising Strong](https://www.amazon.com/Rising-Strong-Ability-Transforms-Parent/dp/081298580X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8\&qid=1536697099\&sr=8-1\&keywords=rising+strong). A stormy first draft is where I sit down and write out all of my feelings and thoughts. It's a place to get it all out - uncensored, unedited. Just take my mind and dump out all my emotions, thoughts, fears, doubts, etc. I go through this process so I can sort through my thoughts and feelings and gain some clarity on the story that I am telling myself. Because that's usually what is going on when I am feeling strong negative emotions (especially emotions like anger, jealousy, hate, fear, etc.) - I am telling myself a story. And then I am looking everywhere for evidence to prove that my story is true. In reality, my story is almost always filled with a lot of half truths or lies and based solely on my emotional responses to unhealthy and unproductive thoughts. Writing everything out can help me to sift through the junk and find the truth. It helps me to identify the [thoughts that aren't serving me](/are-your-thoughts-serving-you/) and then intentionally choose new thoughts that will get me where I want to go. **Record goals and progress** I also use my journal as a place to record my goals and progress with those goals. Growth is often invisible so it is nice to be able to read through my older journal entries and have a tangible way to measure my progress. Life has a way of pushing me forward and giving me new mountains to climb. Because I am constantly facing new challenges and situations in my life, it is sometimes easy to feel like I haven't made any progress and I'm the same (or worse) as I was 10 years ago. I know that this isn't true though. I know that I have made significant progress and growth and its nice to have journal entries to prove it... mostly to myself. I feel gratitude for the growth that I have made and this inspires and motivates me to keep moving forward. **Record decision making process** I like to use my journal as a place to record inspiration that I have received - or ideas that I am trying to decipher and discern - when I am trying to make an important decision. I like to record these things for at least a couple of reasons. First, I find it helpful to have this place to keep track of all of the information that I am gathering as I study out my problem and my thoughts and feelings about how to solve the problem. Second, when the decision has already been made and it is in the past, it is nice to be able to look back at the process that I went through and how everything worked out just as it should (even if it worked out differently than I had anticipated). I feel that it helps me to see more clearly the hand of God in my life. There have been times in my life where I have appreciated being able to look back at past experiences in decision making and those experiences have helped me with future decisions - if anything, just to calm me down and reassure me that God has always been there for me in the past and He won't let me down now. He is always looking out for my best interest. **Recording my learning** I love to learn. I love to read books, listen to talks and podcasts, etc. When I read or hear something that really speaks to me, I have the strong desire to write it down. So that's what I do. I write down what stood out to me and then I write my thoughts and feelings and how this new piece of information inspired me or was exactly what I needed in that moment, etc. Writing these bits of inspiration down help me to solidify the thoughts and feelings in my mind and heart. I am better able to bring these things to remembrance when needed if I have taken the time to sit with them and absorb them into my being. **Record spiritual experiences** -It's important to not only record the problems that I face (stormy first drafts, etc.) but to ALSO record the solutions to those problems. The experiences of being rescued, of being sanctified, of being redeemed. (1 Nephi 1:20) -Sometimes I sit down to write about some thoughts that I had (about a gospel topic or something) and in the act of writing, I receive even more revelation as I write. -spiritual experiences -tender mercies / gratitude -I don’t always journal at the same time of day. And sometimes I journal more than once a day. -receive clearer revelation in the morning -some entries are short, and some are long and detailed -concerns about others reading it or being afraid to write down real thoughts and feelings -journal with the future in mind - for the future me, and for my posterity. I connect the present to the future, knowing that in the future, I will care about the records that I kept in the past. I have shared this before, but something that has helped me with having a consistent journaling habit is typing my entries. I can just type faster than I write. I have used jrnl.com for almost 9 years now. Something that I love about jrnl is that it’s so easy to get my journal entries printed into a book straight through their website. Jrnl also has an app so I can journal anywhere, anytime. Now I love a handwritten journal just like the next guy, so I also do some handwritten journal entries (mostly using The Five Minute journal and our marriage journal.) So do you have to write in a journal everyday to get the benefits? No. It isn’t required. But something that I love about writing everyday is that because the habit is so strong, journaling is something that instinctively comes to mind when I need to do some introspection and reflection, which ends up being really good for my sanity, relationships, and personal growth. -so fun to look back at the progress that I've made during my time with W4LU.
