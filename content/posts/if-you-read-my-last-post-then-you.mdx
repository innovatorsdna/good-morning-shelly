As I have been pondering on all of this throughout this past week, I have been thinking about the importance of feeling. My vision for this focus on feeling has expanded and I've had some insights into why this focus is so important and how it affects my entire life.

At first, I thought that focusing on learning how to deepen my feelings would just increase my level of happiness and improve my relationships. And that is all good and definitely something that will be a result of this focus on feelings. But I'm realizing more and more that learning how to really _feel_ is the most important skill in life. My life (mortal, yes, but especially eternal) actually depends on it.

Let me explain what I mean by that...

Why is it important to be able to really tune into our feelings and to feel _deeply_?

The overarching answer is that the whole point of this life is to learn how to hear and obey the voice of the Lord. The voice of the Lord will guide us and protect us and lead us to the greatest fulfillment and growth. Learning to hear and obey His voice is the answer to everything.

I may not know what I'm supposed to do in any given moment, but God does. I can't see the whole picture. But God does. And I can ask Him for help and guidance. Asking is the easy part (if I remember to ask... still working on that one). Hearing the answer is the tricky part.

> "Yea, behold, I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost, which shall come upon you and which shall dwell in your heart. Now, behold, this is the spirit of revelation." ([Doctrine and Covenants 8:2-3](https://www.lds.org/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/8?lang=eng))

In order to _hear_ the voice of the Lord, through the Holy Ghost, we have to be able to _feel -_ and not just surface level feeling, but deep feeling.

I've been thinking about a few different aspects of this.

First, it's important to really feel the negative feelings that, if tended to, produce positive results. By negative, I am referring to emotions that don't feel good or pleasant, but that are productive. Feelings that we don't necessarily seek out - like sadness, guilt, sorrow, frustration, disappointment, etc.

Being able to really _feel_ is also important when developing and strengthening our testimonies of the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

The more I have been thinking about it this past week, I have been realizing that we can _think_ positively about the gospel, and we can _believe_ that it's true. And that is definitely a step in the right direction. We can make progress with those positive and believing thoughts. But until we really _feel_ the truths of the gospel, deeply, we won't experience a true change of heart - the kind of change that is required for eternal life - a true rebirth. The kind of change that [Alma](https://www.lds.org/scriptures/bofm/alma/5?lang=eng) describes in the Book of Mormon:

> 12 And according to his faith **there was a mighty change wrought in his heart**.
>
> 13 And behold, he preached the word unto your fathers, and **a mighty change was also wrought in their hearts**, and they humbled themselves and put their trust in the true and living God. And behold, they were faithful until the end; therefore they were saved.

I'm going to interject my thought here for a minute... when they experienced the mighty change of heart, the result was that they humbled themselves and put their trust in God. To me, that means that they committed to always listening to and obeying the voice of the Lord. They trusted that He knew the answers way better than they did.

> 14 And now behold, I ask of you, my brethren of the church, **have ye spiritually been born of God?** **Have ye received his image in your countenances? Have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?**

How do we know if we've experienced this mighty change? Alma tells us...

> 15 Do ye exercise faith in the redemption of him who created you? Do you look forward with an eye of faith, and view this mortal body raised in immortality, and this corruption raised in incorruption, to stand before God to be judged according to the deeds which have been done in the mortal body?
>
> 16 I say unto you, can you imagine to yourselves that ye hear the voice of the Lord, saying unto you, in that day: Come unto me ye blessed, for behold, your works have been the works of righteousness upon the face of the earth?
>
> 17 Or do ye imagine to yourselves that ye can lie unto the Lord in that day, and say—Lord, our works have been righteous works upon the face of the earth—and that he will save you?
>
> 18 Or otherwise, can ye imagine yourselves brought before the tribunal of God with your souls filled with guilt and remorse, having a remembrance of all your guilt, yea, a perfect remembrance of all your wickedness, yea, a remembrance that ye have set at defiance the commandments of God?
>
> 19 I say unto you, can ye look up to God at that day with a pure heart and clean hands? I say unto you, can you look up, having the image of God engraven upon your countenances?

To me, what Alma is saying is "How do you _feel_ about God?" Not just "What do you think, or what do you believe to a certain degree," but "How do you really _feel_." In verse 9 of this same chapter (chapter 5), Alma says,

> "And again I ask, were the bands of death broken, and the chains of hell which encircled them about, were they loosed? I say unto you, Yea, they were loosed, and **their souls did expand, and they did sing redeeming love**. And I say unto you that they are saved."

When I read this, the phrase "their souls did expand and they did sing redeeming love" stood out to me. The expansion of soul, to me, points to an increase of depth, an increase of real _feeling_.

Why feel deeply?

-more in tune with the spirit

-more able to feel heart pricks when in need of correction

-more deep desire to do good because you have more of an understanding of the pain and anguish that Christ felt and you have a deeper appreciation for the gift of the atonement

-better able to empathize with others and mourn with them

-don’t just logically know your testimony, but actually feel it. Feelings are more powerful than logic when it comes to standing strong and immovable. And also when it comes to sharing our testimonies. Feeling goes further than logic

Why experience and really feel negative feelings?

-develop empathy and be able to truly connect with others

-feel out of control and have a need and desire to rely more on God

-feel more alive

-buffering and pushing negative emotion away is nice in the moment because it's less uncomfortable/painful. But it's damaging in the long run

I think that some people may claim that they feel emotions very deeply. I'm not accusing them of lying, because I'm not them and I don't know what they feel. But I am not just talking about a physical/emotional depth, I'm talking about a spiritual/emotional depth. I hope that I am able to get the words out in the right way to convey what I mean by that.

So what steps have I been taking towards increasing my depth of feeling?

First, I'm just trying to practice feeling.

-learning how to feel joy (dancing to music, etc.)

-when negative emotion comes up, getting out of my head (ruminating), into my body (processing), and then into my heart (asking questions and listening to the spirit).

-trying to be more conscious and curious of when I am tempted to buffer (mostly with social media)
