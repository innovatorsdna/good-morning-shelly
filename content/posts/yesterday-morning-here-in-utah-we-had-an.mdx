Yesterday morning, here in Utah, we had an earthquake. I was in the schoolroom, reading my scriptures when the house started shaking. I recognized the sensation from last February when we had had an earthquake (3.7 magnitude) - which was the first earthquake I had ever experienced before (that was big enough for me to feel at least). Landon was already in the schoolroom with me, but it woke Porter, Roxanne, and Evelyn up. Turns out that it wasn't actually a small earthquake... it was moderate. 5.7 magnitude... which is the biggest earthquake Utah has had since 1992 (St. George, 5.9). The epicenter was in the Magna area, which is about 37 miles away from our house. The earthquake happened at 7:09 am and there have been 70 aftershocks since then (it's now 3:02 pm). Nanay and Tat felt quite a few of the aftershocks because they are a lot closer to Magna than we are, but I have only felt one of them (which was a 4.6... most of the aftershocks averages around 3.0). Seriously, this past week has been a trial and has caused me multiple times to have to intentionally choose faith over fear. First the coronavirus pandemic, and now earthquakes. I hope this means that the second coming is coming soon. Even before this earthquake, I had been feeling like something was different. Something has shifted. If/when this all blows over and things return to "normal" again, I don't think they'll ever be quite the same as they were before. Like I mentioned yesterday, I feel like something has shifted over this past week. The Lord is moving His work forward, in interesting and peculiar ways. I don't know when the Second Coming of Jesus Christ will be. No one does. But I feel the call even more strongly than I ever have before to wake up and get ready. I ran across this talk this morning and it helped me...
