I just had a baby a few weeks ago, so naturally, for the past several months, I've been thinking a lot about babies, mothers, and motherhood. This has led me to thinking a lot about my Heavenly Mother. I want to think about Her and seek a relationship with Her more. If you look around at everything in nature (including humans), there are parents and children (or offspring). Plants start out as seeds and grow up to be mature plants. Animals start out as babies and grow up to be adults. Humans start out as babies and grow up to be adults. And these plants, animals, and humans reproduce babies that then grow up to be mature plants, animals and people. You can't have a child (plant, animal or human) without parents. And parents nearly always (and are designed to) produce the next generation of children. Its how life works. Carrot seeds mature into carrots. Chicks grow up to become chickens. Lambs grow up to become sheep. Human babies grow up to become mature adults. It makes sense to me then that if I believe that I am a literal child of God (this term includes both my Heavenly Father and Mother), which I do, then I believe that my divine destiny is to grow up (spiritually mentally, emotionally, and physically) and become a God. Children of God grow up to become mature, adult Gods. I guess I should specify here that carrot seeds _have the potential to&#x20;_&#x6D;ature into carrots; chicks _have the potential to&#x20;_&#x67;row up to become chickens; lambs _have the potential to&#x20;_&#x67;row up to become sheep; human babies _have the potential to&#x20;_&#x67;row up to become mature adults; and children of God _have the potential to&#x20;_&#x67;row up to become mature, adult Gods.

I think it’s somewhat safe to project a lot of what She is like based on the experiences that I have in mortal womanhood, wifehood, and motherhood... (although She now experiences these in perfect form.) So with that in mind, I have been thinking about things that I notice about my children’s relationship with me as their mother and things I notice about myself as a mother and what I am naturally drawn to do in regards do my children. Many of these have become a lot more obvious in my mothering now that I have an infant again that is completely dependent on me.

Things I notice about my kids’ relationship with me as their mother:

-they come to me when they are hurt

-they come to me when they want to show me something that they have made

-when Crosby is crying, he almost instantly stops and calms down when I pick him up.

-If Crosby is laying next to me, he will wiggle on our bed until he ends up right next to me. And then he will roll himself over to his side so he can be snuggled facing me

-they want me to watch them...especially when they have learned something new

Things I notice about myself as a mother and what I am naturally drawn to do in regards do my kids:

-calm them when they are crying (as babies and young kids this manifests as me trying to soothe them...their crying is sort of painful to me and I want it to stop so I try to calm them)

-feed them (from my own body for the first couple years of their physical existence, and then through cooking meals for them for many years to follow)...I often even know when my baby needs to eat (my milk lets down) even before he has communicated his hunger

-teach them

-protect them

-clothe them (literally, when babies and toddlers, but still provide clothing for them for many years even though I am not literally the one dressing them)

-warming them

As I thought about these things (as well as the experiences that I had during my recent pregnancy and birth), and also as I read some poems from the book [Mother's Milk: Poems in search of Heavenly Mother by Rachel Hunt Steenblik](https://www.amazon.com/Mothers-Milk-Rachel-Hunt-Steenblik/dp/0998605220/ref=sr_1_2?dchild=1\&keywords=Mother%27s+milk%3A+poems\&qid=1623277872\&sr=8-2), I jotted down some impressions that I had about Heavenly Mother.

DAY-OLD CHILD

My day-old child lay in my arms.

In a whisper, lips to ear—

I said, “Oh, dear one,

How I wish—I wish that you could hear.

“I’ve a hundred wonderful things to say—”

(A tiny cough and a nod)

“Hurry, hurry, hurry and grow—

So I can tell you about God.”

My day-old baby’s mouth was still

And my words only tickled the ear—

But a kind of a light passed through those eyes—

And I saw this thought appear:

“How I wish I had a voice and words—

I’ve a hundred things to say.

Before I forget I’d tell you of God—

I left God just yesterday.”

—Carol Lynn Pearson
