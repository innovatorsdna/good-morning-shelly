We have been full-time homeschooling for 7 months now and overall it has been a beautiful experience. I feel deep gratitude and appreciation that I get to live this life and have these experiences with my children. But it's not _all_ butterflies and rainbows all the time. It is definitely challenging... just like everything that is worthwhile. I felt like I should share a few of my journal entries that express my some of the feelings that I've had. November 11, 2019 I have been thinking lately and trying to figure out why I have felt so challenged by my daily life recently. It's strange because I am so grateful to be able to be homeschooling my kids. I have no regrets in this decision. I feel very blessed to get to do this. There are so many things that I love about it. But yet at the same time, I sometimes get stuck in ruts where I just feel overwhelmed and like I'm drowning. I feel stuck in negativity and expectations. ... more so than I think I used to feel. I kept feeling so confused by this. I didn't understand why my inner world wasn't looking like what I imagined it would be. But I think I am starting to understand the why. Motherhood can be so hard. Since becoming a mom, I have become very aware of my weaknesses. I make a million mistakes everyday. And I realized that because I am with my kids all day, everyday, I am forced to face my weaknesses all day, everyday. I am constantly being tested and tried and put through the furnace. I can't seem to escape my weaknesses. And this is really hard and can be really draining. It’s challenging. It would definitely be a lot easier to only have to face my weaknesses, head on, in the ring, for a few hour every day (before/after school) if my kids were in school all day everyday. I would have time and space away from them. I would have time to forget some of my weaknesses for a while. But at the same time, I feel that not only am I working to build something great (a Christ-centered, refined, and cultured home), but that I too am being built in the process. I am being purified and sanctified through the process... even if it feels like I'm drowning/burning instead. It reminds me of a song that I really like called Cleanse You by Calee Reed The waves come high My ship is sinking And I'm thinking of letting go The waters deep And I am gasping Struggling to hear you, Lord Captain of my soul \[Chorus] Why did you lead me here to this uncharted sea? Did you bring me here just to drown me? "What if I didn't bring you here to drown you but to cleanse you?", He whispers. And "What if you chose to breathe me in? Let this wash you clean a-gain," He whispers. He whispers, "I know the waters deep but if you only knew it isn't meant to drown you," He whispers. "But to cleanse you.” \[Verse 2] The sun sets low My faith is failing And I'm sailing deep into the night The fog rolls in and I am searching Trying in vain again to find Your guiding light (Repeat Chorus) And when I realize and remember that, I wouldn't have it any other way. I don't want to stay the same. I want to be changed and purified and sanctified. I am grateful for this opportunity and blessing to get to homeschool my kids... even though and perhaps because it is super challenging. I'm being built in the process. December 9, 2019 I just realized something... remember when I was writing a few weeks ago (that entry was on November 11th) about how I finally realized why homeschooling (and motherhood) has been so challenging for me (even though at the same time it has been a beautiful and wonderful thing)? I had been struggling with feelings that I couldn't really define and I couldn't really put my finger on why I was feeling down and just blah. And then it dawned on me one day. I figured it out (the next layer of it, at least). I wrote about how homeschooling is challenging mostly because I am with my kids allllll day, every day. I am in the mother/teacher role allll the time. And motherhood has a way of bringing out my weaknesses and making them very apparent. So, homeschooling is challenging because I have to face my weaknesses head on all the time. Figuring this out and being able to define it automatically helped to pull me out of the blah feelings that I had been experiencing. I didn't feel broken anymore. I understood the why and that was comforting. It didn't really solve the problem (my weaknesses), but at least I was able to better understand and not wonder if I was just experiencing a mild form of depression or something... and if it was a mild form of depression, knowing the why helped to pull me out of it. And then, about a week later, (on November 20th) I happened to be on Instagram and I watched a video that Ralphie from Simply On Purpose posted. She knows a lot and teaches about positive parenting. I have been following her for a couple years now I think, and I even saw her speak at the Thriving Moms conference in June 2018, but I often forget to apply what she teaches. Anyway, this particular video was about ignoring junk behavior and she did a bunch of role playing that I found to be super helpful. I showed Jershon the video and we started trying to implement some of the skills that she taught. And I noticed improvement. And then last week, Ralphie launched her audio course where she teaches 4 foundational principles of positive parenting. I debated on purchasing the course or not because it's Christmas time and Christmas is an expensive time of year. But I also felt like this was a worthy endeavor and it would bless me and our family, so I did it. I bought the course. I started listening that same day. The course is only a couple hours long so I listened to it a little here and there over the next few days. And then I immediately started it over again and listened to a little more than half of it for the 2nd time through. Then on Thursday of last week, Jershon and I went on a date. And he happened to mention to me about how he had been struggling with feeling frustrated with the kids lately and he felt like most of his interactions with them in the evenings are filled with frustration. I told him about Ralphie's course that I had been listening to and suggested that he listen to it with me as well. He's not anti parenting resources, but usually he prefers me just summarizing things for him (he has never read any of the parenting books I've suggested), but I think because he was experiencing his own frustrations at the moment and feeling unfulfilled in his role as a father, he was ready to be taught and he happily agreed to listen to the course with me. So that night we started listening the course together (the 3rd time for me). Over the past few days, we've been slowly making it through the lessons of the course and are a little over halfway done with it and we have been working to practice and implement the mindsets and tools. We have had several successful moments over the past few days. Jershon mentioned to me the other night that even though he can't remember everything that we have been learning during the audio course, so far it has been helping him to just remember to be nicer. :) I told him that he doesn't have to remember everything. He can keep listening to the course a little at a time, even for just a few minutes a day, to keep it fresh in his mind and for it to help to change his thoughts (and then his feelings/actions will follow). This afternoon, I was thinking about it and realizing that while yes, being with my kids alllll day brings the challenge of being faced with my weaknesses allll day, but it also gives me tons of opportunities to practice these skills. I would have less opportunities to practice if my kids weren't home with me all day. So I actually feel like I have the opportunity to become more refined, purified, and sanctified than I would if I was only having to practice these skills during small chunks of the day (before and after school). And for that, I'm grateful. Because like I mentioned at the end of my entry on November 11th, I don't want to stay the same. I want to be changed, purified, sanctified, and refined. So I am grateful that I am experiencing lots of opportunities to practice and learn line upon line. This reminds me of a new song that I heard last week. It's called In the Meantime by Jenny Jordan Frogley: In her heart she holds the dreams. That she's carried since the day she turned 13. Of all that she would be when she was grown. So to sum up my thoughts on this (at the moment at least), figuring out the why behind the challenges that I have been facing more heavily off and on over the past couple months helped to make sense of things for me and it was encouraging. And then learning these new positive parenting skills from Ralphie has been really empowering and given me hope that I really can change and my weaknesses can be turned into strengths. And that has been exciting.
