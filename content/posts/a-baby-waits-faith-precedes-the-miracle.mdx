This past year has been so full of life changes and I never really took the time to write any posts about my pregnancy. But now here I am, 3 weeks postpartum and I decided to do a series of 4 posts to share some things about how we got here, my pregnancy, the birth of our baby, and the birth of myself as a new mother. I've gone back and forth about sharing my thoughts and feelings here on my blog because I know that anyone in the world could read this and these experiences are raw, vulnerable, personal, and special. But I keep feeling like I should share so here we go. If you're reading this, be gentle with my heart and brace yourself for a lot of details. And if you pay close attention, you'll notice that the details in this series weave together beautifully. Because God knows how much I love that. :) After the birth of our last baby, Roxanne, in 2016, we were pretty sure that we were done having babies. We didn't make any official decisions about this, but we just thought that our family was probably complete. I had mixed feelings about this. (You can read more of my thoughts about that [here](/feel-2018-loving-with-abandon-feels-like-my-true-home/).) We always said that we were open to revelation about having another baby, if that was God's will for our family, but as the years went by, and I hadn't received any super strong impressions, I got used to the idea of being done with that chapter of life. Although I was sad about this at times, there were upsides as well. The more time that passed, the more sure I felt that we must be done having babies. That chapter was closed, never to be opened again in this life. We settled into the next phase of our family life. No more diapers, bottles, pacifiers, cribs, strollers, sleep deprivation, and high chairs for us. But then in January 2020, I started feeling impressions that perhaps we weren't done after all. These impressions came mostly through dreams. I had a couple dreams myself but I tried not to place much importance in them, thinking that they were just crazy dreams. ... until Evelyn started having dreams as well. At the beginning of January, Evelyn had a dream that we had another baby and it was a girl. She told me that morning. Then she told Jershon that night... he said "mom did have a baby girl... it's this one..." and then he hugged Roxanne. But then Evelyn said "No. In my dream you had 5 kids." My mini panic started building and I wondered if this meant something. But then a few days later, she had another dream. This time I had 3 babies and I named one of them pinky. ... that dream seemed much more on the crazy side so it was easier to dismiss it. haha. But then a few weeks later, I had another dream... and that put me back to the anxiety and back and forth thoughts of "is this a sign? Is Heavenly Father trying to tell me that He wants us to have another baby, that there is another baby waiting for us?" ... or "nah, it's just a dream. I have crazy dreams all the time that don't actually mean anything real.” I also read in my journal that one day in June 2018, Porter made a comment "Roxanne is a big sister to someone in heaven." These dreams, comments, thoughts, and questions weighed heavy on my heart and mind. The impressions became stronger and stronger. I wanted Heavenly Father to just come out and tell me what to do. ...He knew that deep down, I already knew (although I still didn't _know_ that I knew). I knew that there was another baby waiting to come to our family. And that scared me. That scared me for a few reasons... First, I already felt super inadequate as a mom to the 4 kids that I already had. As I am very aware of my weaknesses, I felt like my kids deserved more than I was already giving them and I felt like it would be unfair to them to add another child to our family because it would stretch me even thinner (after making me fatter... haha. :) ... I couldn't resist.) I also didn't want the big age gap between my kids. All the others are 2 years apart and I love that they are so close and are best friends. Second, I was afraid to go through another pregnancy and birth. This seems silly because I haven't had any traumatic experiences with pregnancy and/or birth. I've had pretty great experiences actually (still challenging though). But I guess I still had some fears about me not being able to maintain that track record. Eventually _something_ would go wrong. The other shoe had to drop eventually, right? Not a healthy or hopeful mindset, I realize. haha. Another somewhat real fear that lingered in the back of my mind as well had to do with my blood pressure. When I was pregnant with Roxanne, I experienced white coat syndrome for the first time. Here's a little back story... When I was pregnant with Roxanne, I was still very much working through my [driving fears](/the-gift-of-dawn-fear-of-driving-and-angelic-assistance/) so driving to my first prenatal appointment (15 minutes away, and involving the freeway) was kind of stressful for me. ... on top of just being nervous about my first prenatal appointment and wanting to make sure that there was a real live baby growing inside me. So here I am, just having survived the stress of driving myself to my appointment in a new place that we had only lived in for a few months. When I got to my appointment, the nurse called me back and did the standard weight check. Then she had me sit down and she checked my blood pressure. It was high. ... It wasn't extremely high, but higher than they wanted it to be. The nurse pointed this out to me. What do you think that did for my stress levels? Of course, then I was nervous about my blood pressure...which only made my blood pressure reading higher when the nurse took it again a couple minutes later. I met with one of the midwives for my appointment and she was concerned about my blood pressure reading and said "if it's high again at your next appointment then you will be considered to have chronic high blood pressure and we will have to do some preliminary tests so we have some information in case you develop preeclampsia later on in the pregnancy." So do you think I was super calm and peaceful when I arrived at my appointment the next month... after having driven myself there again? Nope. Now I was feeling the stress of the drive (which involves the impending drive back home), _plus_ the fear that my blood pressure reading would be high again and then I would end up being considered a high risk pregnancy or something. You guessed it. My blood pressure was high again. I asked her if it was possible that I was just experiencing "white coat syndrome" where I get nervous at the doctor's office so my blood pressure goes up. I told her about what I was told at my first appointment and how it stressed me out all month. She said that that was definitely a possibility and I should check my blood pressure on my own a few times throughout the next month so I could get a better idea. But we also agreed that it would be a good idea to do the preliminary testing just in case. So lucky me. I got to get my blood drawn and do a 24 hour urine collection so they could test it and have some preliminary results, just in case. Everything with my test results looked good. At my appointment the next month, my blood pressure was high again when they checked it. But then they checked it a couple minutes later and it had dropped significantly back down into the normal range. I had also taken my blood pressure at home a few times over the past few weeks and it was always normal. I showed those numbers to the midwife and she agreed that my "high blood pressure" readings were most likely due to me being nervous at the doctor's office. This pattern continued. By this point, I had a negative association with the blood pressure cuff at this doctor's office so at every appointment, my results would be high... but my monitoring at home showed that my blood pressure was consistently normal in my everyday life. Luckily the midwives believed me (thanks to my own monitoring at home) and they stopped making a big deal out of it. But the nurses always got nervous when my results were high and it got a little annoying having to always explain to them "yes...it's always high here... but it's fine at home." My least favorite part of those appointments was the 2 minutes of getting my blood pressure checked. Anyway... everything ended up being fine and I didn't have chronic high blood pressure or preeclampsia after all. So... fast forward to 2020 when I started having strong impressions about potentially having another baby. I was nervous about having to face the blood pressure issue again. At the end of January, on the way to the temple one night, I braved the conversation with Jershon... not that I was necessarily nervous about his response, but that speaking it out loud would make it more real. Like me, Jershon had mixed feelings about the idea of adding another baby to our family. When we were in the Celestial room, I was praying and I told Heavenly Father that I was willing to have more children if that was His will for us and I asked Him to make it known to us, either way. At this point, I was _willing_...but not necessarily to the point of _desiring_ to bring another child into our home. After going to the temple that night, the strong thoughts and impressions kind of died down for a while and I put the idea of another baby on the back burner in my mind. It still surfaced occasionally but didn't seem pressing. I began to think, "ok, well, I told Heavenly Father that I was willing, but He hasn't really given me any strong promptings since then, so either it's not the right time, or we really are done and I just needed to tell Him that my heart was _willing_." As he was quoting this scripture, I thought of the experience that I had this morning during the closing song, Come Thou Fount. These words came into my mind - from the Lord to me - "Give me your heart and I will give you a new one." Immediately after I heard the Lord say this to me, Elder Rasband said "yet, even new hearts may be prone to wander, prone to leave the God we love." Hearing him quote the very part of Come Thou Fount that had been my soul's prayer this morning really hit me. The Lord knows me. He loves me. This message (which I am going to study and apply) from Elder Rasband was specifically for me." ..................................................................................................................................................................... Fast forward to May... and my period was late. I typically have a very regular cycle and we had not been _trying_ to have a baby at this point so my period being late was surprising. As the days passed and my period still hadn't shown up, I was surprised to notice that my thoughts and feelings were not of anxiety (that I had felt other times at the thought of a potential pregnancy if my period was ever a day or so late). Instead I felt peace and calm... even excitement at the possibility of me being pregnant. I also felt that since we hadn't been _trying_, it would have just been an act of God, and an answer to my prayer. If I just ended up with a surprise pregnancy, then I would know that it really was God's will for our family and that would take the responsibility and decision making stress away from me. haha. I don't love having the weight of these decisions on me so this would be nice. I felt the fear being replaced by faith. I felt my heart changing from feelings of "I'll do what God wants me to do... but I may not be all that happy about it at first" to "I think that I want this too. I think I actually _want_ to bring more children (or at least the next one) into our family." As the days passed, I became more and more convinced that I was pregnant, but I wanted to wait until Mother's day (which was only a few days away) to take the pregnancy test because I just thought it would be fun to find out on Mother's day. Well... on the evening of the 7th day, 3 days before Mother's day, as I was making dinner I felt some cramping, and then there was blood...and then a wave of a lot of tears and emotions. ...which didn't really make sense, seeing that I hadn't even been _trying_ to get pregnant in the first place. If my period would have come on time (or even a couple days late), I would have happily welcomed it. But yet the more days that passed, the more my heart was set on this, the more my mother heart grew and I _wanted_ that baby that I was so convinced was growing in me. And now here I was and I felt like I had experienced a loss. I didn't know where to go from there or what to think. I'm going to insert my journal entry here from May 8, 2020. Again, this feels vulnerable to share my very raw and personal thoughts and feelings so openly, but I feel that someone else may benefit from them so here goes...

> I took a pregnancy test last night and another one this morning... just to be sure. Because if it came up positive, that might change how I view this bleeding and cramping that I'm having. They both came up negative. Looks like I was never actually pregnant. :( I guess I just made it all up in my head. Just more evidence that my feelings deceive me and can't always be trusted. I cried a lot last night. My eyes feel really sore and tired now. I can't guarantee that I won't cry some more still. Grief is weird like that. It hits you in waves. You think you're finally ok and ready to move on and then it hits you again. It feels weird to be experiencing grief for something as simple as this. From the outside it makes no sense. "So, your period came and you weren't actually pregnant... you weren't trying to get pregnant anyway! What's the big deal? You should be celebrating!" But that's not how I feel. When we decided that Roxanne would be our last baby (most likely), I felt so sad. I went through a real period of mourning. Of course motherhood is much more than just the pregnancy, birth, and baby phase. And I'll never stop being a mother to the children that I already have. But it felt so weird to be "done" when I wasn't even 30 yet. I had always pictured myself having more and more babies until I just couldn't have them anymore because I was too old. I always wanted _a lot_ of kids... and 4 just doesn't seem like a lot. Of course, once I actually _had_ kids and experienced how challenging it is, it became much easier for me to feel like taking the easy route and not having as many kids as I always planned on. And Jershon felt the same way. But that didn't change the fact that I was born to be a mother. And the thought of not ever having any more babies in this life was really painful. Well, over the past few years, I've just tried to stuff those feelings and put them to the side. No use crying over something that's done and over. Just move on. Focus on the kids I already have (which is obviously a good thing and they definitely deserve even more of my love and attention than I give to them). You can't just keep having babies forever anyway. Eventually _someone_ has to be your last baby. But something about it just didn't ever feel settled. As evidenced by some dreams, random comments from the kids, and journal entries, I have just often felt torn and wondered if our family really is all the way complete or not. And how am I to know?!?! Every time I asked God, I didn't ever seem to get any concrete answers. Fast forward to this current experience... The thoughts and feelings I had in January... and then this supposed _surprise_ pregnancy (that ended up being made up in my head). I've never been 7 days late on my period before....not when I wasn't nursing still or just barely becoming regular again after weaning. How could I _not_ have assumed that I could be pregnant? And even though I felt kind of nervous about it, most of me felt like it was a gift. Like God was giving us a gift and telling us that we weren't done having babies. And that was a balm to my soul. I would get another chance to experience the magic and meaning of motherhood to another soul. Yes, it would be hard and require a lot of sacrifice, but it would be so worth it just like it's been with our other 4 children. And then last night happened...and all those dreams were ripped away from me. It was like the wound (caused from being "done" after Roxanne) that had been covered up and forgotten about (well, I _tried_ to forget about it) was ripped open again. Why?!?! Was this just a random natural bodily thing that happened because of Covid-19 stress? Did it just throw off my cycle? ....because that is very possible. Or does it mean something? Is this experience trying to carry a message to me? Is Heavenly Father trying to tell me something? Was this experience just meant to really open up my heart to inviting more children into our home? Was this a test of my desires and of my heart and of my faith? And now He's giving _me_ the opportunity to consciously and intentionally _choose_ to bring another child (or more) into our home? ... because honestly, having God decide for me and just send us a surprise baby would have been easier for me. haha. I don't like all of this "you make the decision" stuff. I would rather just have God give me an obvious sign that I couldn't misunderstand. Especially because 95% of the time, the decision rests on _me_...especially with these big family choices. Jershon will support me, but almost every big decision for our family has rested on my shoulders because I am usually the one that receives the promptings first and strongest when it comes to these kinds of things. And sometimes that feels really heavy and daunting. It's scary stepping into the dark and unknown and feeling fully responsible for how it turns out because it was _my_ choice to begin with. It feels especially hard when I already struggle with not knowing if I can actually trust my personal revelation experiences or not. ...when I wonder if I'm just being deceived and thrown off course. I don't know. How am I supposed to know? And yet, part of me thinks that I do know. I think the answer is just to fully put my faith and trust in God...every month. To not prevent pregnancy, be willing to invite more children, and see if He wants to send us any more children. But that feels so vulnerable and scary. It feels like voluntarily putting myself on a monthly roller coaster when I am always wondering if _this_ is the month when I'm going to get pregnant (and turn my world upside down... in the best kind of way...but still really hard), or if _this_ month, my life will just keep going like normal. But I guess that's what true faith is. It's believing that God truly knows me and is aware of me and is with me every step of the way. It's believing that things aren't random. ...especially things as vitally important as sending spirits to the earth to get bodies. It's believing that God is in control. It's surrendering my desire for control and giving myself fully to Him. “Believe that your life will be infinitely better if you rely on God to guide your steps. He knows things you cannot possibly know, and He has a future prepared for you that you cannot possibly imagine. … “You may not see it until much later, but you will look back and know that the Lord did indeed direct and guide your path.” (Dieter F. Uchtdorf, “The Adventure of Mortality” (worldwide devotional for young adults, Jan. 14, 2018))

That experience in May was a turning point for me. Through that experience my will and desires were aligned with God's. Over the next few months, we gradually put our faith and trust more fully in God's plan for our family and at the beginning of September, I took a pregnancy test...

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_3869.jpeg)

Then Evelyn said to Roxanne, very matter of factly, "go downstairs and tell daddy that mommy just found out that she is going to have another baby!" haha. Heavenly Father really did send me so many messages about this baby through my kids, which I think is really interesting and special.
