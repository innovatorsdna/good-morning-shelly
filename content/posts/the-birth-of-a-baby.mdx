This is post number 3 in the series about pregnancy and birth. Start with [post #1](/a-baby-waits-faith-precedes-the-miracle/) if you haven't read the beginning of the story yet. As I have been working on writing up the baby's birth story, I wanted to include as many details as possible (mostly for my own record keeping purposes). So here it is. It starts a few days before the birth because I had a bit of prodromal labor...although not as consistent and frequent as I had with Evelyn and Roxanne's labors. Some of these are notes that I wrote in real time (those are written with the bullet points), as they were happening. The rest of the details were filled in afterward. The more specific details (exact wording from someone in the room) came from the video recording of the birth. And just to fill you in... I tend to have the labor pattern of having a long early labor stage (sometimes covering multiple days of prodromal labor) and take a long time to progress to 3 cm dilated. But once I hit 3 cm, my body just takes off and I speed through the active labor/transition/pushing phases within a few hours time. This birth followed that pattern. I had a bit of prodromal labor in the days preceding the birth, but if I'm just counting the time from when labor started officially (meaning my contractions stuck around and didn't stall or stop), I was in early labor for 26 hours, and active labor (for me this is 3 cm and beyond) for a little over 4 hours. Ok... here are the detailed details... **Friday, May 7, 2021** (5 days before birth)

- Yesterday I was feeling like labor might still be days away because I had hardly felt any noticeable or mentionable contractions over the past couple days
- 1:55 am went to the bathroom and on my way back to bed, had a contraction that felt like the real deal. Today may not be the day of the birth, but this feels like it’s the beginning of labor, even if the birth is still a few days away (after some prodromal labor)
- Contractions continuing to come like clockwork every 10 ish minutes (currently 4:00 am). There were several times when I would glance at the clock (feeling like it was probably about time for the next contraction), see that it had been almost 10 minutes since the last one, and then I would feel the contraction begin to start. Some somewhat mild, others much more moderate.
- As the contractions came, I would just surrender myself to them. I just kept repeating “I surrender. This is bringing me closer to the birth of my baby. My body is opening. I will get to meet my baby soon.”

(when I was experiencing some prodromal labor in the middle of the night on April 26th, I didn't want it to be the real thing because it felt so early and I still had so much to get done before the baby came... during those contractions, I had a hard time relaxing. I felt a lot more fear and tension. ... partially because they were accompanied by back pain that didn't even go away between them, and partially because I felt fear about the time when active labor would happen (if that was then, or later) because I wasn't even handling these smaller contractions very well mentally or physically. I was feeling fear and apprehension about being able to have another successful unmedicated birth...so this time it was good for me to surrender to the contractions. It was good for me mentally and physically. It took away the tension and helped me to feel excited about the contractions instead.)

- Feel general achy-ness / crampy-ness in my lower uterus area, even in between contractions
- Around 3 am, I distinctly felt a hand on my shoulder/upper back. I thought it was Jershon putting his arm on me at first - it felt that real - with the weight and warmth. But it wasn’t Jershon. Then I felt as if a few more hands were placed on my shoulder / upper back as well. I heard the words in my mind “we are here with you.” I can’t say for certain but I think these were a few angels -probably my ancestors - communicating to me that I am not alone and that they will be here with me throughout this whole birth journey.
- These consistent, every 10 minutes (average), moderate contractions happened like clockwork from 2 am - 7 am when I decided to get up.
- After I got up and moving for the day, I continued to have contractions for a while but they weren’t consistent like clockwork anymore. Some were closer together, some further apart, but they weren’t as strong in intensity and sometimes it was hard for me to recognize if I was having a contraction or not because they weren’t as obvious. I was tired from being up most of the night so I took a couple of naps while sitting on my birth ball. I was also grumpy with the kids about the messes in the house because I was feeling the urge to nest and get the house a little more in order...and I was tired, plus feeling a little disappointed that my contractions had pretty much stopped. But after a while I pretty much gave up on the notion that today was going to be the day that I gave birth. I settled into staying pregnant for a while longer. Most likely just day 1 of prodromal labor. We’ll see how many days I have of this before the baby decides to come. Prodromal labor seems to be my pattern. It’s got it’s pros and cons.
- I have continued to have contractions off and on throughout the day, but nothing consistent or worth timing. Some have been more mild. Others more moderate. I am currently having a more moderate one while the baby is moving around and feels like it’s jabbing at my pelvis. That’s not that comfortable.

**Sunday, May 9, 2021** (3 days before birth)

- Really wanted to go into labor and have the baby today. I thought it would be fun to have the baby on Mother’s Day... and also I had always thought of today as my potentially real due date because of the ultrasounds that I had at 8 and 21 weeks where both ultrasounds measured the gestational age and calculated the due date to be May 9th.
- No sign of labor coming anytime soon so in the late afternoon, I decided to try out the Miles Circuit to see if I could get the baby in an optimal position and see if that would start things up.

The day ended and I gave up the idea of a Mother’s Day baby **Monday, May 10 2021** (2 days before birth)

- Went to my midwife appointment in the afternoon. I was curious to see if I was making any progress so I had my midwife check my cervix (for the first time... usually I start getting checked at around my 37 or 38 week appointments but this time I just didn’t feel like I needed to.) I was dilated to a 2 (which I was pleasantly surprised to hear... I was expecting a 1), 50% effaced, and -2 station. I told her that I had been having lots of contractions, but nothing that seemed to be moving me towards active labor. My midwife said that the baby just isn’t in the best position to move things along and get labor started. She suggested that I crawl around on my hands and knees and do exaggerated left side lying.
- When I got home, I got on my hands and knees for as long as I could stand it and then I spent some time on my birth ball, leaning over my bed.
- When I went to bed I slept on my left side with a pillow between my legs, keeping the bottom leg straight and the top leg bent.

**Saturday, May 8 - Monday, May 10 2021** (4-2 days before birth) Kept expecting to have more prodromal labor like I had with Evelyn and Roxanne but I didn’t seem to be following that pattern as closely as I thought I would. I had lots of contractions but they weren’t in consistent patterns and they felt like a mixture of painless Braxton Hicks and then more moderately strong early pre labor contractions. With this inconsistent mixture and frequency pattern, I just tried to ignore my contractions for the most part, feeling like they weren’t doing anything anyway so there was no point in getting excited about them. **Tuesday, May 11, 2021** (1 day before birth)

- Started having contractions about every 13 minutes or so around 3 am ish. I timed them for a while, sleeping in between, but then stopped timing and just tried to sleep as much as I could. They were mild/moderate but noticeable enough.
- Got up around 6:15 am to get dressed and see if my contractions would continue or taper off. I got a water bottle and filled it with water so I could rule out dehydration as a cause for the contractions. I sat on my birth ball and leaned over onto my bed so that I could relax but also watch the Come Follow Me lesson from Unshaken. I drank my water as I continued to have contractions over the next couple hours... eventually averaging about 9 minutes apart. Still mostly mild to moderate in intensity.
- Asked Jershon to give me a blessing, in case this was the beginning of the real deal.
- 10:55 am... Contractions still coming every 10-15 minutes. Intensity increasing a bit compared to earlier but still manageable
- Been laying in bed, sitting on my birth ball, on my knees, leaning over my birth ball, and listening to my birth meditations (the one that I wrote, and the ones that were from my online birth class).
- Went to the bathroom at about 11:45 am (for like the 10th time this morning since I have been drinking so much water). I wiped myself and looked at the toilet paper (like I’ve been doing a lot lately) to see if there were any chunks of mucus plug. I’ve only had a few pretty small pieces come out over the past week. I didn’t see anything on the toilet paper (which has been the norm) but then I got up, turned around to flush and saw a big glob of mucus plug on the toilet seat that must have fallen there when I stood up. It wasn’t bloody, but definitely a much bigger and obvious chunk than I have been losing up to this point. I was excited to see that because it gave me hope that things are at least headed in the right direction, even if birth is still a ways away
- 2:15 pm contractions still 10-15 minutes apart but the last 3 were definitely stronger in intensity than most have been for the past 11 hours
- My contractions seem to be stronger when I am laying in bed on my side (in exaggerated left side lying position)
- Around 5:30 pm, texted this to Jershon “ When you’re done, will you try some rebozo sifting? I think this baby just isn’t all the way in the right position and that’s what’s keeping things from picking up and progressing. Been having 10-15 apart minute contractions for 14 hours now.” We tried the rebozo sifting technique and I think it might have helped.
- I have been giving my mom the updates throughout the day and telling her to be ready to come, just in case. Eventually we decided that it would be best for her to come up so she drove up and got here around 6:30 pm. I hope that she didn’t drive all that way for no reason (if my labor stalls or stops.)
- Ended up drinking 100 oz throughout the day. Had to pee a lot. Haha.

Since my contractions STILL weren’t picking up in frequency and it didn’t look like we would be heading to the hospital anytime soon, we watched episode 4 of The Chosen that was just released tonight. I had some stronger contractions while we were watching that made me wonder if things would start progressing more. One contraction in particular was really strong but I think it was extra strong because my bladder was full because after I peed, the next contractions weren’t as intense. After the show, we put the kids to bed. My contractions were still coming every 10-15 minutes and I had a feeling that maybe they would continue to pick up and perhaps we would end up going to the hospital sometime before midnight or maybe in the middle of the night. I told the kids that maybe they would wake up to the news of the baby being born. (I hoped it wasn’t just wishful thinking.) Went to bed but still kept timing my contractions as they continued to come every 10-15 minutes. Fell into a deeper sleep just before midnight. **Wednesday, May 12, 2021&#x20;**(Birth day) From midnight to 1 am, I had an hour break in contractions (or at least they were mild enough for me to sleep through them so I didn’t wake up to time them) but when they came back, they were still pretty strong so it showed me that that little break wasn’t my contractions slowing down or stopping, but just a little tender mercy so I could get some rest. Started timing them again and they were still 10-15 minutes apart. 2:00 am... I sat up in bed, debating over and over in my mind if we should go to the hospital or not. According to the frequency of my contractions (still mostly 10-15 minutes apart like they had been for 23 hours by this point), it didn’t make sense according to the “textbook labor” to go to the hospital. But the intensity or my contractions had been stronger since about 7 or 8 pm the night before and were slowly but progressively getting stronger. I didn’t want things to pick up rapidly at home and also I was starting to wonder if the frequency wasn’t progressing because my body didn’t feel safe to continue with frequency and intensity since I wasn’t in my birth place yet. 2:45 am... I decided to call the on call doctor from my midwife’s office to see what advice they could give me and if they thought I should come in to labor and delivery to get checked out. The on call doctor said that I could come in if I wanted to be checked and since I am already 40 weeks, they could help me along if I needed/wanted (which I didn’t even consider because I didn’t want to use any medical induction methods.) I told her that I would see what the next few contractions were like and then I would probably come in to get checked out. She said she would call labor and delivery to let them know. I paced in our room for the next couple contractions and then I had one that was a little less than 6 minutes apart from the last one so I told Jershon that we better get packed up and head to the hospital. I think this closer contraction was meant to give me the kick in the pants that I needed so that I would decide that going to the hospital was the best idea. ... because after this one, my contractions went back to being about 8-10 minutes apart (which was improvement over the 10-15 minutes, but still not close enough together to warrant going to the hospital according to the “textbook” We loaded up the van with the things we were taking to the hospital and then headed out.

In my birth preparations this time, I learned about the breast crawl. This is how wikipedia describes it: "Breast crawl is the instinct of mammal newborns to move towards the nipple and attach to it for breastfeeding all by themselves. In humans, if the newborn baby is put on the mother's abdomen, the movements start 12 to 44 minutes after birth, followed by spontaneous suckling at 27 to 71 minutes after birth." I really wanted to try this out this time so after they put Crosby back on my chest, skin to skin this time. It was hard to get him down very low, but he was calm for a little bit and then kicked around and maneuvered his way up to my breast. He bobbed his head around for a while and eventually (pretty quickly actually... I didn't time it, but I think it only took him 20 minutes or so) got really close to latching on. I helped him out a bit by repositioning a little bit but for the most part he did it completely on his own. And then he latched on and started nursing! It was pretty amazing. Since then, he has had a really good latch and we haven't had any latch issues. I can't say for sure, but I think that the breast crawl was a big part of helping him to have such a good latch and it got our nursing relationship started off in a great way. I wish I had known to do this with my other babies.

After that, we pretty much spent the rest of the time at the hospital doing skin to skin. We didn't get Crosby dressed (besides a diaper) until right before we left to go home on Friday late morning. I was wearing my hospital gown and/or nursing gown the whole time that we were in the hospital and I would just tuck him into the front of my gown so he could nurse and then sleep on me for most of the time. I loved having so much skin to skin time with him and again, this is something that I wish I would have done more of with my other babies but I just didn't know much about it at the time. Jershon did a little bit of skin to skin time with him as well. After Crosby nursed really well for quite a while, the baby nurse took him over to the warmer to get his vitals and stats. If him being a boy wasn't surprise enough, we were shocked to see how big he was! He weighed 7 lbs, 6 oz (our first baby over 7 lbs) and was 20.5 inches long (an inch taller than Evelyn, who was our tallest at birth). His head also measured 14 inches... which is big (but not _too&#x20;_&#x62;ig) for a newborn... let alone a Lopez newborn. haha. All of my other babies heads have been about 13 inches at birth. I definitely felt his big head during the pushing process and even my midwife (before finding out his head measurement) said that he had a big round head.

![](/uploads/2021/05/IMG_1423.jpeg)

They continued to monitor my blood pressure off and on throughout the time that I was at the hospital. I had some good readings and some higher readings. Because of the higher readings, my midwife had my blood drawn a few different times to get a preeclampsia panel done just to make sure that I wasn't experiencing preeclampsia. All 3 times, my results came back just fine. So they finally settled on diagnosing me with gestational hypertension... which was kind of annoying because here I had gone my entire pregnancy without any real blood pressure issues and then on the day of labor/delivery, I get diagnosed with gestational hypertension (which is better than preeclampsia, so I should count my blessings). ... more on this in the next post. Because of the blood pressure stress, I didn't feel like I got to really enjoy my time at the hospital, just soaking in the new baby bliss, this time. The anxiety of the blood pressure thing was always in the back of my mind. I tried not to let it worry me too much. Sometimes I was successful with that, other times I wasn't. But even with all of that, I was grateful to have had a great, mostly uncomplicated birth experience and to have a healthy, adorable baby. We wanted our kids to be the first ones to know the baby's gender and since they didn't want to know until they met him/her in person (which would be at home because of Covid restrictions at the hospital), Jershon and I were the only ones that knew the baby's gender for the first 2 days of his life. It was hard not to say any gender specific pronouns when we video chatted with my mom and the kids when we were in the hospital. haha.
