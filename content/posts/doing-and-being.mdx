Something that I have been thinking about lately is how I am really good at _doing_. I feel like I have a really good work ethic. I get stuff done. I work hard. That was instilled in me from a very young age. It's in my blood.

I think that this strength of mine is very important. Work is important. Living the gospel requires a lot of work. Personal growth takes a lot of work.

But I am realizing more and more that that's only one side of the coin.

The thing that I am lacking in is really having a _relationship_ with God. I rely too heavily on work and not enough on faith. I rely too much on my own abilities and my own willpower and not enough on the atonement of Jesus Christ. I often forget to _ask_ God for help.

I am noticing this weakness of mine even more as my kids get a little older. The other night when it was one of the boys' turn to say the family prayer, he just rattled off a few quick things that he is "grateful" for and then ended the prayer. The problem was that these are the SAME things that he always says. We told him that he needed to think of some other things to say and then asked him to say the prayer again. He was a tiny bit more thoughtful, but again, only really said things that he's "grateful" for. (grateful is in quotes because I don't think he really _feels_ the gratitude for them, he just says those things because they are just habits). And then I realized and started to explain to him that it's great to be grateful for our things that Heavenly Father has given us, and that is really important, but...

1\. we need to actually feel that gratitude, not just say the words because we're supposed to, and

2\. Heavenly Father wants us to _ask_ for His help. When we don't ask Him for anything, it reveals our pride. It shows that we think that we think that we can just handle everything on our own. We think that we don't _need_ him.

The sad thing is, I know that my kids have learned that from me.

But I don't want it to be this way.

I want my kids to have a true relationship with God. I want a real relationship with God for myself as well. And I know that working on this in myself first, will help me to be able to be a better example to my kids. I don't want them to just go through the motions and do what they have to do. I feel like that comes from fear. That if we don't do the right things then we will get punished or not get our reward or something. But that shouldn't be our motivation.

I was listening to a conference talk by President Dieter F. Utchdorf yesterday morning called [Perfect Love Casteth out Fear](https://www.lds.org/general-conference/2017/04/perfect-love-casteth-out-fear?lang=eng). This part stood out to me...

> "First, let us address the problem with fear. After all, who among us has never been compelled by fear to eat better, wear a seat belt, exercise more, save money, or even repent of sin?
>
> It is true that fear can have a powerful influence over our actions and behavior. But that influence tends to be temporary and shallow. **Fear rarely has the power to change our hearts, and it will never transform us into people who love what is right and who want to obey Heavenly Father.**

> **People who are fearful may say and do the right things, but they do not feel the right things.** They often feel helpless and resentful, even angry. Over time these feelings lead to mistrust, defiance, even rebellion."

Here's another quote from Joseph F. Smith...

> “We do not preach the gospel of fear. We do not seek to terrorize the souls of men. We do not ask a man to be righteous because of the terrors of the damned. We do not want you to be good because you fear the punishment of the wicked. We do not want you to do right because of the penalty that attaches to the doing of wrong. **We want you to choose the right because it is right, and because your heart loves the right, and because it is choice above everything else.** We want you to be honest, not merely because it is the best policy, but because in so doing you honor God and you carry out His purposes in your lives; for “an honest man,” it is an old, and perhaps a hackneyed, saying—“is the noblest work of God.” We want to be honest because we love God, and we cannot be the Saints of God \[unless] we are. We should be good because we love to be good, and not because we fear the consequences of evil.

> The Lord does not accept obedience from men except that which they render cheerfully and gladly in their hearts, and that is all that is desired by his servants. That is the obedience we ought to render, and if we do not we are under condemnation.” ([Source](https://www.lds.org/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-32?lang=eng#19-35744_000_036))

So how do I change this? And what is it that needs to change? Because I know that _doing_ is really important. I feel like it even takes _work_ to have faith. And vice versa, it takes some faith to put in the work.
